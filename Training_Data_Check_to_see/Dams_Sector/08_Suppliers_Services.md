## 3. Spare Parts Suppliers (30+ Patterns)

### 3.1 OEM Parts vs. Aftermarket

**PARTS-001: OEM Spare Parts Strategy**
```
ADVANTAGES OF OEM PARTS:
- Guaranteed fit: Exact specifications
- Warranty: Manufacturer warranty included
- Quality: Original manufacturing standards
- Documentation: As-built drawings, certifications
- Liability: OEM responsibility for defects

DISADVANTAGES:
- Cost: 20-50% higher than aftermarket
- Lead time: 12-52 weeks for custom parts
- Availability: Discontinued models difficult
- Vendor lock-in: Single source dependency

CRITICAL OEM PARTS (Recommended):
- Turbine runner: $2M-$10M, 18-36 month lead time
- Generator rotor: $1M-$5M, 12-24 months
- Wicket gate servomotor: $50K-$500K, 6-12 months
- Governor control system: $100K-$1M, 6-12 months
- Protection relays: $5K-$50K each, 8-16 weeks
```

**PARTS-002: Aftermarket Parts Strategy**
```
ADVANTAGES OF AFTERMARKET:
- Cost: 30-60% savings vs. OEM
- Availability: Faster delivery, stock items
- Competition: Multiple suppliers drive prices down
- Flexibility: Custom modifications possible

DISADVANTAGES:
- Quality risk: Variable quality standards
- Warranty: Limited or no warranty
- Liability: Unclear responsibility for failures
- Compatibility: Fit and function not guaranteed

SUITABLE AFTERMARKET PARTS:
- Bearings: Standard sizes (SKF, Timken, FAG)
- Seals: O-rings, gaskets, mechanical seals
- Filters: Oil filters, air filters, water filters
- Fasteners: Bolts, nuts, washers (ASTM/SAE grades)
- Cables: Power cables, control cables, fiber optics
- Sensors: Standard temperature, pressure, level transmitters

CAUTION AREAS:
- Safety-critical: Governor components, protection relays
- Proprietary interfaces: Vendor-specific communications
- Obsolete equipment: Reverse-engineering required
```

### 3.2 Critical Spares Inventory

**SPARES-001: Recommended Spare Parts List**
```
CATEGORY 1: EMERGENCY SPARES (On-site, immediate need)
Turbine:
- Wicket gate seals: 2 sets per unit
- Labyrinth seal segments: 1 set per unit
- Bearing pads (thrust, guide): 1 set per bearing
- Governor oil filters: 4 each
- Position sensors (LVDT, encoders): 2 each critical sensor

Generator:
- Bearing shells (guide, thrust): 1 set per bearing
- RTD temperature sensors: 10 assorted
- Cooling water filters: 4 each
- Air filter elements: 4 each
- Fuses (control circuits): Assortment

Auxiliaries:
- Pump seals (cooling, drainage): 2 each pump type
- Motor starter coils: 2 each starter size
- Circuit breaker mechanisms: 1 per critical breaker
- PLC backup (entire CPU and I/O rack configuration)

CATEGORY 2: PLANNED OUTAGE SPARES (Warehouse, lead time <6 months)
- Governor servomotor seals and gaskets
- Excitation system thyristors
- Coupling bolts and hardware
- Oil pumps (governor, bearing)
- Cooling fans (generator, transformers)
- Voltage regulator modules
- Protection relay (spare relay, same model)

CATEGORY 3: LONG-LEAD SPARES (24+ months procurement)
- Spare turbine runner (if critical, unique design)
- Generator rotor (catastrophic failure spare)
- Main transformer (if not easily replaceable)
- Penstock sections (if damage likely, e.g., seismic zone)
- Spillway gate components (unique, large items)

INVENTORY MANAGEMENT:
- Stock rotation: Use oldest parts first (FIFO)
- Shelf life: Monitor elastomers, electronics
- Storage conditions: Climate-controlled, moisture-free
- Documentation: Inventory database, traceability
- Audits: Annual physical inventory counts
```
