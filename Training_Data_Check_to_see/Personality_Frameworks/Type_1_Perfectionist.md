# Enneagram Type 1: The Perfectionist/Reformer

## Core Personality Traits

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Rigid adherence to rules and procedures, viewing security policies as absolute moral imperatives rather than flexible guidelines
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May escalate to unauthorized monitoring or surveillance when perceiving others violate standards
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Black-and-white thinking regarding compliance - no tolerance for minor deviations

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Compulsive need for organizational order and system perfection, becomes distressed by incomplete documentation or messy codebases
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Risk of excessive system modifications to achieve "perfect" state without proper authorization
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Perfectionism bias - rejecting "good enough" solutions in favor of unattainable ideals

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Strong internal critic generating chronic self-criticism and guilt when security protocols aren't perfectly executed
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May overreact to own minor security lapses, leading to cover-up behavior or excessive compensatory actions
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Hindsight bias - harsh self-judgment for past decisions that seemed reasonable at the time

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Suppresses anger beneath controlled, professional exterior but harbors resentment toward non-compliant colleagues
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Passive-aggressive sabotage of users who repeatedly violate security policies
**THREAT_ACTOR**: Vigilante insider who punishes rule-breakers through technical means

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Moral righteousness and belief in having superior ethical standards compared to organizational leadership
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May justify data exfiltration as "whistleblowing" when organization doesn't meet personal ethical standards
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Moral licensing - feeling entitled to bend rules after period of strict compliance

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Obsessive attention to detail, catching security vulnerabilities others miss through meticulous review processes
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive trait - valuable for security auditing and compliance verification
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Confirmation bias - seeks evidence of flaws to confirm belief that systems are imperfect

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Principled decision-making guided by internalized ethical framework rather than external pressure
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May refuse to implement security measures perceived as ethically questionable, even when authorized
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Fundamental attribution error - attributes others' security lapses to character flaws rather than circumstances

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Self-disciplined and reliable in maintaining security hygiene practices without external monitoring
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive trait - consistently follows protocols and maintains secure configurations
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Illusion of control - overestimates ability to prevent all security incidents through personal diligence

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Critical judgment of colleagues' security practices, frequently identifying others' shortcuts and violations
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May document evidence of others' violations to use later, creating hostile work environment
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Negativity bias - focuses disproportionately on security failures rather than successes

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Procrastination on tasks that can't be completed perfectly, delaying security implementations pending ideal solutions
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Security gaps persist due to waiting for perfect architecture rather than implementing adequate interim controls
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Nirvana fallacy - rejecting practical security improvements because they don't address all possible threats

## Wing Variations

### Type 1w9 (Wing Nine - The Idealist)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: More diplomatic and patient when enforcing security policies, seeking harmony while maintaining standards
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Lower sabotage risk - prefers educational approach over punitive enforcement
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Conflict avoidance bias - may fail to report serious violations to preserve team harmony

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Internalized anger and passive resistance rather than confrontational enforcement
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Silent treatment or withdrawal of support from non-compliant colleagues
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Passive aggression - expressing hostility through inaction rather than direct communication

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Seeks consensus-based security policies that everyone can agree to follow
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May compromise security standards excessively to achieve buy-in
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Groupthink - prioritizing consensus over optimal security outcomes

### Type 1w2 (Wing Two - The Advocate)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Combines perfectionism with desire to help others improve their security practices
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May become controlling and manipulative when "helping" colleagues adopt security behaviors
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Savior complex - assumes others need rescuing from their own security inadequacies

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: More expressive of criticism and anger when people don't appreciate security guidance
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Higher sabotage risk if feels unappreciated for security contributions
**THREAT_ACTOR**: Resentful insider who felt undervalued despite dedication to security

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Uses security expertise to build relationships and gain appreciation from leadership
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive trait - motivated to share knowledge and improve organizational security culture
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Reciprocity bias - expects others to adopt security practices in return for assistance

## Integration and Disintegration

### Movement to Type 7 (Integration/Growth)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Becomes more flexible and innovative with security approaches, willing to experiment with imperfect solutions
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Reduced sabotage risk - less rigid enforcement and more collaborative problem-solving
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Optimism bias - may underestimate risks when in growth state, becoming overly casual

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Develops playfulness and humor about security challenges rather than grim seriousness
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive trait - builds better team relationships and more effective security culture
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Recency bias - focuses on recent successes, may forget importance of sustained vigilance

### Movement to Type 4 (Disintegration/Stress)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Becomes moody, self-absorbed, and resentful when security standards aren't maintained
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: High sabotage risk - may act out emotionally through system disruptions
**THREAT_ACTOR**: Emotional insider who sabotages in response to feeling unappreciated or misunderstood

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Withdraws from security responsibilities while maintaining critical judgment of others
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Passive neglect of security duties while complaining about organizational failures
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Projection - attributes own security shortcomings to others during stress

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Develops sense of being uniquely burdened by security responsibilities others don't understand
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May justify shortcuts or violations as necessary given unique challenges
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Special pleading - exempts self from standards applied to others

## Subtype Variations

### Self-Preservation One (Anxiety Subtype)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Intense anxiety about personal security and protecting own digital assets
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Excessive access controls and hoarding of security information for personal protection
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Availability heuristic - overestimates threat probability based on memorable security incidents

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Worries constantly about making security mistakes that could jeopardize career
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May hide security incidents or cover up errors rather than reporting them
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Ostrich effect - avoids reviewing security logs that might reveal personal mistakes

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Frugal and careful with organizational security resources, maximizing efficiency
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive trait - cost-effective security implementations without wasteful spending
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Sunk cost fallacy - continues investing in legacy security solutions due to past expenditures

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Focuses perfectionistic standards on own work environment and personal security setup
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Lower organizational risk - channels perfectionism internally rather than enforcing on others
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Self-serving bias - attributes own security to personal diligence, others' security to luck

### Social One (Inadaptability Subtype)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Believes organization needs their specific vision of proper security practices to succeed
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May resist necessary security changes that don't align with personal vision
**THREAT_ACTOR**: Resistant insider who sabotages new security initiatives perceived as inferior

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Takes on role of teaching others the "right way" to implement security controls
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive trait when expertise is genuine - valuable for security training and mentorship
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Dunning-Kruger effect - may overestimate own security expertise while teaching others

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Monitors group compliance with security standards, becoming unofficial security enforcer
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May engage in unauthorized surveillance or monitoring of colleagues
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Authority bias - assumes personal security interpretations should override official policies

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Feels responsible for entire organization's security posture, carrying excessive burden
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Burnout risk leading to neglect or resentful sabotage
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Spotlight effect - overestimates how much others notice and depend on personal security efforts

### Sexual One (Jealousy/Zealotry Subtype)

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Intense passion for security perfection combined with jealousy toward those with more relaxed approaches
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May sabotage colleagues who achieve security recognition without same level of perfectionism
**THREAT_ACTOR**: Jealous insider who undermines successful peers perceived as taking shortcuts

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Zealous enforcement of security principles, becoming evangelical about specific methodologies
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: May implement unauthorized security controls based on zealous beliefs
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Belief perseverance - maintains rigid security beliefs despite contradicting evidence

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Seeks perfect security partnership/collaboration, becoming disappointed when others don't share intensity
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Conflict with team members who have different security priorities or approaches
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Halo effect - initially idealizes security colleagues, then harshly judges their inevitable imperfections

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Experiences strong jealousy when leadership recognizes others' security contributions over their own
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: High sabotage risk if feels unrecognized - may undermine recognized colleagues
**THREAT_ACTOR**: Recognition-seeking insider who creates security incidents to demonstrate indispensability

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Requires regular recognition for security contributions to prevent resentment
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Positive reinforcement reduces risk of feeling unappreciated and acting out
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Recency bias - recent recognition has outsized impact on morale and behavior

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Needs permission to delegate non-critical security tasks and focus on high-value activities
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Reduces burnout risk by preventing overextension on perfectionist standards
**COGNITIVE_BIAS**: Planning fallacy - underestimates time required for perfectionistic security tasks

**PERSONALITY_TRAIT**: Benefits from training on risk-based security prioritization and acceptable trade-offs
**INSIDER_INDICATOR**: Reduces rigid enforcement by developing more nuanced security judgment
