## Technical Capabilities

Manufacturing processes include core construction using cold-rolled grain-oriented silicon steel with step-lap or mitred-lap joint configurations achieving core loss below 0.9 W/kg at 1.7 Tesla. Winding technology employs continuous disk winding for high voltage applications with paper-covered copper conductor achieving 0.75% impedance tolerance. Insulation systems use thermally upgraded Kraft paper with mineral oil achieving 105°C average winding rise and 130°C hot spot temperature. Vacuum oil filling removes moisture and gases achieving less than 10 ppm water content and 0.5% dissolved gas by volume.

Testing facilities perform routine tests including turns ratio, polarity, insulation resistance, applied voltage, induced voltage, and load loss measurements per IEEE C57.12.90 standards. Type tests validate impulse withstand capability applying 1.2/50 microsecond full wave and chopped wave impulse voltages verifying insulation coordination. Partial discharge testing measures corona activity below 100 picocoulombs at 1.5 times rated voltage ensuring insulation quality. Audible sound testing confirms noise levels below 65 dBA at rated load meeting environmental requirements.

Training programs deliver classroom and hands-on instruction covering transformer operation, maintenance procedures, and troubleshooting techniques.

Lifecycle support services offer dissolved gas analysis interpreting gas concentrations identifying incipient faults including thermal heating, arcing, and cellulose degradation. Oil reclamation processes remove moisture, particulates, and dissolved gases extending oil service life by 20+ years. Remanufacturing programs include core and coil replacement, tank refurbishment, and cooling system upgrades extending transformer operational life. Monitoring system installation includes Qualitrol Transformer IQ providing continuous dissolved gas analysis, moisture measurement, and thermal monitoring with remote access capability.
