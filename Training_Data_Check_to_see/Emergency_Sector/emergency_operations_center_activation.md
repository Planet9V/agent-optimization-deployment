# Emergency Operations Center Activation Operations

## Overview
Operational procedures for activating, staffing, and operating Emergency Operations Centers (EOCs) during disasters and major incidents coordinating multi-agency response under the Incident Command System and National Incident Management System.

## Annotations

### 1. EOC Activation Criteria and Levels
**Entity Type**: ACTIVATION_PROTOCOL
**Description**: Defined thresholds and decision criteria triggering EOC activation at various operational levels
**Related Entities**: Emergency Response, Scalable Activation, Resource Mobilization
**Technical Context**: Level 1 (monitoring), Level 2 (partial), Level 3 (full activation), trigger indicators, watch officer decision
**Safety Considerations**: Timely activation, appropriate level, scalability, false activation costs, delay risks

### 2. ICS/NIMS Implementation in EOC
**Entity Type**: ORGANIZATIONAL_STRUCTURE
**Description**: Incident Command System structure implementation within EOC including command, operations, planning, logistics, and finance sections
**Related Entities**: NIMS Compliance, Command Structure, Organizational Framework
**Technical Context**: EOC organizational chart, ICS-200/300/400 training, span of control, unified command, area command
**Safety Considerations**: Clear authority, role clarity, accountability, scalable structure, succession planning

### 3. Multi-Agency Coordination Center Setup
