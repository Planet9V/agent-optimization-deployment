## Chemical Sector

**Total Subsectors:** 5 segments

The Chemical Sector is an integral component of the U.S. economy that manufactures, stores, uses, and transports potentially dangerous chemicals on which other critical infrastructure sectors rely. The sector converts raw materials into more than 70,000 diverse products essential to modern life and distributes those products to more than 750,000 end users throughout the nation.

### Subsector Breakdown

1. **Basic Chemicals**
   - Description: Fundamental industrial chemicals used as building blocks for other products
   - Role: Raw materials and intermediates for manufacturing processes
   - Infrastructure: Large-scale chemical plants and manufacturing facilities

2. **Specialty Chemicals**
   - Description: High-value chemicals with specific performance or functional properties
   - Role: Used in specific applications requiring precise characteristics
   - Infrastructure: Specialized manufacturing facilities

3. **Agricultural Chemicals**
   - Description: Chemicals for agricultural production including fertilizers and pesticides
   - Role: Support food production and agricultural operations
   - Infrastructure: Production and distribution facilities serving agricultural sector

4. **Pharmaceuticals**
   - Description: Medicinal and pharmaceutical products
   - Role: Healthcare and medical treatment applications
   - Infrastructure: Pharmaceutical manufacturing plants, research facilities

5. **Consumer Products**
   - Description: Chemical products for consumer use including household goods
   - Role: Direct consumer applications in daily life
   - Infrastructure: Consumer goods manufacturing and distribution facilities

### Sector Scale
- **Products:** More than 70,000 diverse chemical products
- **End Users:** More than 750,000 end users nationwide
- **Economic Impact:** Essential to modern economy and national security

## Commercial Facilities Sector

**Total Subsectors:** 8 subsectors

The Commercial Facilities Sector comprises sites that draw large crowds of people for shopping, business, entertainment, or lodging. These facilities are typically open to the public and operate with significant public access. Most facilities in this sector are privately owned and operated.

### Subsector Breakdown

1. **Entertainment and Media**
   - Description: Facilities for entertainment production and distribution
   - Examples: Motion picture studios, broadcast media facilities
   - Public Access: Variable, some facilities have restricted areas

2. **Gaming**
   - Description: Gambling and gaming facilities
   - Examples: Casinos, gaming establishments
   - Public Access: Open to general public (age restrictions apply)

3. **Lodging**
   - Description: Temporary accommodation facilities
   - Examples: Hotels, motels, conference centers, RV parks, campgrounds
   - Public Access: Customer access with reservations

4. **Outdoor Events**
   - Description: Large-scale outdoor venues and events
   - Examples: Theme parks, amusement parks, fairs, parades, exhibitions, public parks, marathons
   - Public Access: Fully public or ticketed events

5. **Public Assembly**
   - Description: Indoor venues for large gatherings
   - Examples: Arenas, stadiums, aquariums, zoos, museums, convention centers
   - Public Access: Ticketed or general admission facilities

6. **Real Estate**
   - Description: Commercial and residential building infrastructure
   - Examples: Office buildings, apartment buildings, condominiums, mixed-use facilities, self-storage
   - Public Access: Tenant/owner access with varying security levels

7. **Retail**
   - Description: Commercial shopping facilities
   - Examples: Retail centers and districts, shopping malls
   - Public Access: Fully open to general public

8. **Sports Leagues**
   - Description: Professional and organized sports organizations
   - Examples: Professional sports leagues and federations
   - Public Access: Administrative facilities restricted; events open via ticketing

### Sector Characteristics
- **Ownership:** Predominantly privately owned and operated
- **Access Pattern:** Open public access with large crowd attraction
- **Security Challenge:** Balance security with open access requirements
- **Economic Impact:** Significant employment and economic activity

## Communications Sector

**Total Subsectors:** 5 primary infrastructure modes

The Communications Sector provides an "enabling function" across all critical infrastructure sectors as identified by Presidential Policy Directive 21. Over the last 25 years, the sector has evolved from predominantly a provider of voice services into a competitive and interconnected industry using terrestrial, satellite, and wireless transmission systems. The infrastructure is predominantly privately owned and operated.

### Subsector Breakdown

1. **Broadcast Infrastructure (Broadcasting)**
   - Description: Free, over-the-air radio and television transmission systems
   - Services: Analog and digital audio programming, video programming, data services
   - Infrastructure: Broadcast towers, transmission facilities, studios
   - Access: Public free-to-air service

2. **Cable Networks (Cable)**
   - Description: Wired broadband network infrastructure
   - Services: High-speed Internet access, video programming, digital telephone service
   - Infrastructure: Cable distribution systems, headends, fiber-optic and coaxial networks
   - Access: Subscription-based service

3. **Satellite Communications (Satellite)**
   - Description: Space-based telecommunications platforms
   - Services: Voice, video, and data signal relay as part of telecommunications networks
   - Infrastructure: Satellites in orbit, ground stations, uplink/downlink facilities
   - Access: Subscription or commercial service

4. **Commercial Wireless Networks (Wireless)**
   - Description: Mobile and radio-based communication systems
   - Services: Cellular phones, paging, personal communication services, high-frequency radio, unlicensed wireless, commercial and private radio services
   - Infrastructure: Cell towers, base stations, mobile switching centers
   - Access: Subscription-based mobile service

5. **Wireline Networks (Wireline)**
   - Description: Fixed terrestrial telecommunications infrastructure
   - Services: Public phone traffic, wide and local area data traffic, Internet traffic
   - Infrastructure: Fiber-optic and copper-based networks, central offices, switching centers
   - Access: Subscription-based landline and data service

### Sector Characteristics
- **Interconnected Nature:** Satellite, wireless, and wireline providers depend on each other to carry and terminate traffic
- **Facility Sharing:** Companies routinely share facilities and technology to ensure interoperability
- **Internet Support:** Transport networks support the Internet as critical infrastructure
- **Ownership:** Predominantly private sector owned and operated
- **Evolution:** Transformed from voice-centric to multi-service digital platforms
- **Economic Impact:** Essential enabling infrastructure for all other sectors

## Cross-Sector Analysis

### Sector Risk Management
- **Chemical Sector SRMA:** CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency)
- **Commercial Facilities Sector SRMA:** CISA
- **Communications Sector SRMA:** CISA (Department of Homeland Security designated)

### Common Characteristics
1. **Private Ownership:** All three sectors are predominantly privately owned and operated
2. **Critical Infrastructure:** Recognized as vital to national security and economic security
3. **Public-Private Partnership:** CISA leads sector partnerships for security and resilience
4. **Interdependency:** All sectors depend on communications infrastructure for operations

### Sector Interdependencies
- **Chemical ↔ Communications:** Chemical facilities require communications for monitoring, control systems, and emergency response
- **Commercial Facilities ↔ Communications:** Rely on communications for operations, security, payment processing
- **Chemical ↔ Commercial:** Chemical products support commercial facility operations (cleaning, maintenance)
