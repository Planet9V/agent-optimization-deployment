## Fire Detection and Suppression Equipment

### Smoke Detectors
**Photoelectric Smoke Detectors**
- Light-scattering technology
- Detects smoldering fires (better than ionization)
- Addressable or conventional
- 2-wire or 4-wire operation
- Low-profile design
- LED indicator (alarm status)
- Removable smoke chamber (easy cleaning)
- Drift compensation (reduces false alarms)

**Ionization Smoke Detectors**
- Radioactive material (Americium-241)
- Detects fast-flaming fires
- Less common in modern installations
- Conventional (non-addressable)
- Fixed-temperature backup sensor
- Battery-powered (residential models)
- Interconnectable (hardwired or wireless)

**Dual-Sensor Smoke Detectors**
- Combines photoelectric and ionization
- Broad fire detection spectrum
- Reduces false alarms (both sensors must activate)
- Addressable models
- Multi-criteria detection algorithms
- Faster fire detection

**Aspirating Smoke Detection (ASD)**
- Vesda (Xtralis/Honeywell)
- FAAST (Honeywell)
- TITANUS (Bosch)
- Air sampling pipes throughout space
- Very early warning (incipient stage detection)
- Sensitivity levels: Very high, high, normal, low
- Applications: Data centers, museums, clean rooms
- Laser-based smoke detection
- Clean agent compatible

### Heat Detectors
**Fixed-Temperature Heat Detectors**
- 135°F (57°C) - standard rating
- 190°F (88°C) - high-temperature areas
- Bimetallic element or fusible link
- Non-restorable (one-time use) or restorable
- Low-profile design
- Addressable or conventional
- Applications: Kitchens, garages, attics, mechanical rooms

**Rate-of-Rise Heat Detectors**
- Detects rapid temperature increase (15°F/min)
- Pneumatic (air chamber expansion)
- Electronic rate-of-rise
- Combination (fixed-temperature + rate-of-rise)
- Addressable models
- Faster response than fixed-temperature alone

**Linear Heat Detection Cable**
- Heat-sensitive cable (100-300 feet)
- Detects heat along entire cable length
- Applications: Cable trays, conveyors, tunnels, warehouses
- Fixed-temperature or rate-of-rise
- Resettable or non-resettable
- Zone modules for location identification

## Power and Electrical Equipment

### Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS)
**UPS Technologies**
- **Online Double-Conversion**: Continuous power conditioning (APC, Eaton, Vertiv)
- **Line-Interactive**: AVR (Automatic Voltage Regulation), battery backup
- **Standby (Offline)**: Battery kicks in on power failure
- **Modular UPS**: Scalable capacity (add modules as needed)
- **Lithium-Ion UPS**: Lighter, longer life than VRLA batteries

**UPS Capacities**
- Small UPS: 500VA-3kVA (desktop, workstations)
- Medium UPS: 5kVA-20kVA (server rooms)
- Large UPS: 40kVA-500kVA (data centers, facilities)
- Mega UPS: 1MVA+ (large data centers, hospitals)

**UPS Features**
- Battery backup runtime (5-30 minutes typical)
- Extended battery modules (additional runtime)
- Automatic bypass (if UPS fails)
- Static bypass switch (manual maintenance bypass)
- Hot-swappable batteries
- LCD display (status, alarms, metrics)
- Network management card (SNMP, web, email alerts)
- Input/output voltage and frequency regulation
- Harmonic filtering (reduces electrical noise)

**UPS Manufacturers**
