## Abstract

Group dynamics are not linear; they are topological. A group of 2 (Diad) is fundamentally different from a group of 3 (Triad). In the **McKenney-Lacan Theorem**, we model small groups as **Simplicial Complexes**. We demonstrate that the **Diad** is the unit of *Intimacy/Conflict*, while the **Triad** is the unit of *Structure/Stability*. We apply **Knot Theory** (specifically the Borromean Knot) to model the Lacanian RSI structure of a resilient cyber defense team, proving that a team of 3 is the minimal stable unit for complex decision-making.

## 1. The Diad (1-Simplex)

A Diad consists of two nodes $A$ and $B$ connected by an edge $E_{AB}$.
$$ \Delta_1 = \{A, B\} $$

### 1.1 The Instability of the Diad
In a Diad, there is no mediator.
*   **Mirror Dynamics**: $A$ sees itself in $B$. If $A$ projects aggression, $B$ reflects it.
*   **Equation of State**: $S_{AB} = \sigma_A \cdot \sigma_B$.
    *   If $\sigma_A = \sigma_B$ (Resonance), energy is low.
    *   If $\sigma_A \neq \sigma_B$ (Dissonance), there is no third party to absorb the tension. The Diad breaks.

### 1.2 Cyber Application: Pair Programming
*   **Pros**: High bandwidth, immediate feedback.
*   **Cons**: High risk of "Groupthink" (Ferromagnetic alignment) or "Deadlock" (Antiferromagnetic alignment).

## 2. The Triad (2-Simplex)

A Triad consists of three nodes $A, B, C$ and the face $F_{ABC}$.
$$ \Delta_2 = \{A, B, C\} $$

### 2.1 The Stability of the Triad (Simmel's Law)
Georg Simmel argued that the Triad is the first stable social form.
*   **The Mediator**: If $A$ and $B$ fight, $C$ can mediate.
*   **The Coalition**: If $A$ is aggressive, $B$ and $C$ can form a coalition to check $A$.

### 2.2 The Borromean Knot
We model the ideal Cyber Defense Triad as a **Borromean Knot**.
*   Three rings are linked such that if *any one* is cut, the other two fall apart.
*   **Ring 1 (Real)**: The Incident Responder (Deals with the raw trauma of the breach).
*   **Ring 2 (Symbolic)**: The Policy Analyst (Deals with the law/compliance).
*   **Ring 3 (Imaginary)**: The PR/Comms Officer (Deals with the narrative).

**Theorem**: A defense team that lacks any one of these rings (R, S, or I) is topologically unstable and will fail during a Seldon Crisis.
