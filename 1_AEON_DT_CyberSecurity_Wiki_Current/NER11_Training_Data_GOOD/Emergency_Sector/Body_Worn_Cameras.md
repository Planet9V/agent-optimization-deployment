## BWC Policies and Best Practices

### Recording Policies

#### When to Record (Model Policy)
- **Law Enforcement Encounters**: All law enforcement interactions with public
- **Traffic Stops**: All traffic stops from initial contact to completion
- **Arrests**: All arrests and custodial situations
- **Searches**: All searches of persons, vehicles, buildings
- **Use of Force**: All uses of force (mandatory recording)
- **Pursuits**: Vehicle or foot pursuits
- **Calls for Service**: Response to calls for service
- **Interviews**: Field interviews and witness statements
- **Crime Scenes**: Crime scene documentation
- **Transports**: Prisoner transports

#### When NOT to Record (Privacy Considerations)
- **Victims of Sexual Assault**: Officer discretion for victim privacy
- **Medical Emergencies**: Inside hospitals, medical facilities (HIPAA)
- **Administrative Activities**: Non-enforcement administrative tasks
- **Informants**: Confidential informant meetings (officer safety)
- **Undercover Officers**: Encounters with undercover officers
- **Locker Rooms**: Locker rooms, restrooms (privacy)
- **Schools**: Inside schools (unless emergency or enforcement)
- **Officer Requested**: Officer may request supervisor approval to stop

### Activation and Deactivation
- **Manual Activation**: Officer presses record button to start
- **Pre-Event Buffer**: Camera continuously buffers last 30-120 seconds
- **Auto-Activation Triggers**: Automatic start via triggers:
  - Lightbar activation (emergency lights)
  - Siren activation
  - Weapon holster draw (Bluetooth holster sensor)
  - Radio transmission (Bluetooth from P25 radio)
  - CAD dispatch notification (CAD integration)
  - Door opening (vehicle door sensor)
- **Deactivation**: Officer ends recording at conclusion of event
- **Supervisor Override**: Supervisors can remotely activate cameras

### Video Retention Policies
- **Evidentiary Video**: Retained for statute of limitations period (7-10 years typical)
- **Non-Evidentiary Video**: Retained for 60-180 days
- **Use of Force**: Retained indefinitely or per policy (often 7+ years)
- **Critical Incidents**: Retained indefinitely (officer-involved shootings, deaths)
- **FOIA Requests**: Retained until FOIA request resolved
- **Ongoing Investigations**: Retained until case closed
- **Purging**: Automated purging after retention period expires
- **Legal Hold**: Videos under legal hold exempt from purging

### Access and Review
- **Officer Review**: Officers may review their own videos before report writing
- **Supervisor Review**: Supervisors review videos for training, investigations
- **Investigations**: Internal affairs access for investigations
- **Prosecutor Access**: Secure sharing with prosecutors for case review
- **Defense Access**: Discovery sharing with defense attorneys
- **Public Records**: FOIA/public records requests with redaction
- **Audit Logging**: All access logged with user, date, time, purpose

### Redaction Requirements
- **Faces**: Blur or pixelate faces of bystanders, victims (privacy)
- **Minors**: Redact faces of juveniles (privacy laws)
- **Nudity**: Redact nudity, medical privacy
- **License Plates**: Redact license plates of uninvolved vehicles
- **Home Interiors**: Redact personal information visible in homes
- **Audio**: Mute or redact sensitive audio (victim statements, medical info)
- **Automated Redaction**: AI-powered automatic face detection and blurring
- **Manual Review**: Human review of automated redactions for accuracy
