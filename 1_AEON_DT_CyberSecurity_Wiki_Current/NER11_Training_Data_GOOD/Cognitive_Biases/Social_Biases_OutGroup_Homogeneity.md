# Out-Group Homogeneity Bias - Social Perception Training

## Overview
Out-group homogeneity bias leads to perceiving members of other groups as more similar to each other than members of one's own group, enabling social engineering attacks that exploit tribal thinking and stereotyping.

## Core Manifestations

### 1. Tribal Identity Exploitation
<COGNITIVE_BIAS category="social_perception" severity="high" exploitation_vector="tribal_manipulation">
Out-group homogeneity creates exploitable "us vs them" mentality where attackers leverage tribal identities to bypass critical thinking about individual threats within perceived out-groups.
<cross_reference type="PERSONALITY_TRAIT">tribalism_susceptibility</cross_reference>
<cross_reference type="SOCIAL_ENGINEERING">identity_based_attacks</cross_reference>
</COGNITIVE_BIAS>

### 2. Vendor Trust Stereotyping
<COGNITIVE_BIAS category="organizational_trust" severity="high" exploitation_vector="vendor_impersonation">
Organizations treat "trusted vendor" category as homogeneous, assuming all communications from vendor domain are equally legitimate, enabling sophisticated vendor impersonation attacks.

## Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Vendor Homogeneity Attack
Employee receives email from "@trusted-vendor.com" domain requesting credential reset for "all vendor portal users." Out-group homogeneity causes employee to assume all vendor communications are equally legitimate without verifying specific vendor representative.

### Scenario 2: Department Stereotype Exploitation
Attacker claims to be "from HR" requesting employee information, exploiting assumption that all HR staff follow identical processes without verifying individual HR representative identity or specific process requirements.

### Scenario 3: Geographic Trust Pattern Attack
