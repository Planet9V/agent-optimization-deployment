## TIER STRUCTURE & STRATEGIC RATIONALE

### TIER 1: Cognitive Biases (100 files, 60 hours)
**Strategic Value:** HIGHEST - Foundation for decision-making accuracy

**Why First:**
- Cognitive biases fundamentally distort threat perception and response
- Every analyst must understand decision-making distortions
- Establishes baseline for all subsequent entity extraction
- 8 psychological entities are the most abstract - need full focus

**File Categories:**
- Confirmation Bias
- Anchoring Bias
- Dunning-Kruger Effect
- False Consensus Effect
- Availability Heuristic
- Sunk Cost Fallacy
- In-Group Bias
- Hindsight Bias

**Quality Gate:** 100% validation, IAA >0.85

---

### TIER 2: Incident Reports (145 files, 96 hours)
**Strategic Value:** HIGH - Real-world ground truth

**Why Second:**
- Incident data is authentic evidence of how biases manifest in attacks
- Technical entities are grounded in real attack chains
- Maximizes model generalization to new incidents
- Provides context for relationship mapping

**File Categories:**
- APT Campaigns & Attribution
- Ransomware Case Studies
- Data Breaches & Exfiltration
- Supply Chain Attacks
- ICS Incidents
- Zero-Day Exploits
- Malware Analysis
- Threat Actor Profiles

**Quality Gate:** 75-50% validation, IAA >0.80

---

- Validates threat patterns across all 16 sectors
