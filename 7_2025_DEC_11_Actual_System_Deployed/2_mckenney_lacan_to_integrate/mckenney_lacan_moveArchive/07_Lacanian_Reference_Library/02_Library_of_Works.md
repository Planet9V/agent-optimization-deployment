# Library of Lacanian Works

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This document serves as a chronological and thematic index of Jacques Lacan's teaching, primarily divided between his oral Seminars and his written texts (*Écrits*).

## The Seminars (1953–1980)

Lacan's teaching was primarily oral. The official text was established by Jacques-Alain Miller.

### The Early Teaching (Language & The Symbolic)
*   **Book I: Freud's Papers on Technique** (1953–1954)
    *   Focus: Resistance, transference, and the basics of analytic technique.
*   **Book II: The Ego in Freud's Theory and in the Technique of Psychoanalysis** (1954–1955)
    *   Focus: The machine, cybernetics, the "Big Other," and the decentering of the ego.
*   **Book III: The Psychoses** (1955–1956)
    *   Focus: Foreclosure, the Name-of-the-Father, and the Schreber case.
*   **Book IV: The Object Relation** (1956–1957)
    *   Focus: Lack, Phallus, and the maternal object.
*   **Book V: The Formations of the Unconscious** (1957–1958)
    *   Focus: Wit, the Graph of Desire (early versions), and the paternal metaphor.
*   **Book VI: Desire and its Interpretation** (1958–1959)
    *   Focus: Hamlet, fantasy ($ \diamond a $), and the structure of desire.
*   **Book VII: The Ethics of Psychoanalysis** (1959–1960)
    *   Focus: Antigone, Das Ding (The Thing), sublimation, and the "duty" of desire.

### The Middle Teaching (The Object *a* & Logic)
*   **Book VIII: Transference** (1960–1961)
    *   Focus: Plato's Symposium, the "agalma."
*   **Book IX: Identification** (1961–1962)
    *   Focus: Topology (torus), unary trait, and the logic of the signifier.
*   **Book X: Anxiety** (1962–1963)
    *   Focus: **The Object _a_** is finally isolated. Anxiety is "not without an object." The Gaze and Voice.
*   **Book XI: The Four Fundamental Concepts of Psychoanalysis** (1964)
    *   *Founding text of the École Freudienne de Paris.* Unconscious, Repetition, Transfer, Drive.
*   **Book XII: Crucial Problems for Psychoanalysis** (1964–1965)
    *   Focus: Syntax, linguistics, and the position of the analyst.
*   **Book XIII: The Object of Psychoanalysis** (1965–1966)
    *   Focus: Perspective, the Velasquez painting *Las Meninas*, and logic.
*   **Book XIV: The Logic of Phantasy** (1966–1967)
    *   Focus: The traversal of the fantasy, the axiom "There is no sexual relation."
*   **Book XV: The Psychoanalytic Act** (1967–1968)
    *   Focus: The "Pass" (becoming an analyst), revolution (May '68 context).

### The Late Teaching (The Four Discourses & Sexuation)
*   **Book XVI: From an Other to the other** (1968–1969)
    *   Focus: The structure of the Other, Pascal's wager.
*   **Book XVII: The Other Side of Psychoanalysis** (1969–1970)
    *   Focus: **The Four Discourses** (Master, University, Hysteric, Analyst). Entropy and surplus-jouissance.
*   **Book XVIII: On a Discourse That Might Not Be a Semblance** (1971)
    *   Focus: Literature, Chinese writing, and the nature of "semblance."
*   **Book XIX: ...or Worse** (1971–1972)
    *   Focus: Logic, "YAD'LUN" (There is One), and the foundations of sexuation.
*   **Book XX: Encore** (1972–1973)
    *   Focus: **Feminine Jouissance**, the formulas of sexuation, "The Woman does not exist." love and language.

### The Final Teaching (Topology & The Borromean Knot)
*   **Book XXI: Les non-dupes errent** (1973–1974)
*   **Book XXII: R.S.I.** (1974–1975)
    *   Focus: The Knot of Real, Symbolic, and Imaginary.
*   **Book XXIII: The Sinthome** (1975–1976)
    *   Focus: James Joyce, the 4th ring (Sinthome) that upholds the knot.
*   **Book XXIV: L'insu que sait de l'une-bévue s'aile à mourre** (1976–1977)
*   **Book XXV: The Moment to Conclude** (1977–1978)
*   **Book XXVI: Topology and Time** (1978–1979)
*   **Book XXVII: Dissolution** (1980)

## Écrits (Selected Major Texts)

Lacan's *Écrits* (1966) is a dense compilation of essays.

### Key Theoretical Texts
1.  **The Mirror Stage as Formative of the I Function** (1949)
    *   Theory of the ego formation, image, and aggressivity.
2.  **The Function and Field of Speech and Language in Psychoanalysis** (The Rome Discourse, 1953)
    *   The manifesto of the "Return to Freud." Full speech vs. empty speech.
3.  **The Agency of the Letter in the Unconscious or Reason Since Freud** (1957)
    *   Saussurean linguistics applied to Freud. Metaphor (Condensation) and Metonymy (Displacement).
4.  **On a Question Prior to Any Possible Treatment of Psychosis** (1958)
    *   Commentary on Schreber. The Foreclosure of the Name-of-the-Father.
5.  **The Direction of the Treatment and the Principles of Its Power** (1958)
    *   Critique of ego psychology. The analyst pays with words, person, and judgment.
6.  **The Signification of the Phallus** (1958)
    *   The Phallus as a signifier, not an organ.
7.  **Subversion of the Subject and the Dialectic of Desire** (1960)
    *   Complex text explaining the complete **Graph of Desire**.

*   **L'étourdit** (1972) - Known for its extreme linguistic density and topological focus.
