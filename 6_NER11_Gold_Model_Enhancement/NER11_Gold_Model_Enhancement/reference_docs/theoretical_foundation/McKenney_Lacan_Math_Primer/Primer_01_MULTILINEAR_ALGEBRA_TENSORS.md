## 1. Vector Spaces and Dual Spaces

### 1.1 Definition
A **Vector Space** $V$ over a field $K$ (usually $\mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$) is a set closed under addition and scalar multiplication.
*   **Basis**: A set $\{e_1, \dots, e_n\}$ such that any $v \in V$ can be written uniquely as $v = v^i e_i$ (Einstein Summation).

### 1.2 The Dual Space ($V^*$)
The **Dual Space** $V^*$ is the set of linear maps $\alpha: V \to K$.
*   **Dual Basis**: $\{e^1, \dots, e^n\}$ such that $e^i(e_j) = \delta^i_j$ (Kronecker Delta).
*   **Covectors**: Elements of $V^*$ are called covectors (or 1-forms).

## 2. Tensor Products

### 2.1 Definition
The **Tensor Product** $V \otimes W$ is the vector space generated by symbols $v \otimes w$, subject to bilinearity.
$$ (v_1 + v_2) \otimes w = v_1 \otimes w + v_2 \otimes w $$
$$ (\lambda v) \otimes w = \lambda (v \otimes w) = v \otimes (\lambda w) $$

### 2.2 Tensors of Rank $(p, q)$
A tensor $T$ of rank $(p, q)$ is a multilinear map:
$$ T: \underbrace{V^* \times \dots \times V^*}_{p} \times \underbrace{V \times \dots \times V}_{q} \to K $$
In components:
$$ T = T^{i_1 \dots i_p}_{j_1 \dots j_q} e_{i_1} \otimes \dots \otimes e_{i_p} \otimes e^{j_1} \otimes \dots \otimes e^{j_q} $$

### 2.3 Application to Psychometrics
The **Psychometric Tensor** $\mathbf{P}$ is a rank-2 tensor ($1, 1$) mapping Context ($V$) to Behavior ($V$).
$$ \mathbf{P}^i_j = \text{Probability of Behavior } i \text{ given Context } j $$

## 3. Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

### 3.1 The Eigenvalue Equation
For a linear operator $A: V \to V$ (a $(1, 1)$ tensor):
$$ A v = \lambda v $$
*   $\lambda$: Eigenvalue (The scaling factor).
*   $v$: Eigenvector (The invariant direction).

### 3.2 Spectral Decomposition
If $A$ is symmetric (Hermitian), it can be diagonalized:
$$ A = \sum_i \lambda_i |v_i\rangle \langle v_i| $$
*   **Psychohistory Meaning**: The "Eigen-Personalities" are the fundamental, orthogonal modes of behavior. Any complex personality is a superposition of these modes.

## 4. Metric Tensor ($g_{ij}$)

### 4.1 Definition
A **Metric Tensor** $g \in V^* \otimes V^*$ defines the inner product (geometry) of the space.
$$ \langle u, v \rangle = g_{ij} u^i v^j $$

### 4.2 Raising and Lowering Indices
The metric allows us to convert vectors to covectors (Musical Isomorphism).
$$ v_i = g_{ij} v^j \quad (\text{Lowering}) $$
$$ v^i = g^{ij} v_j \quad (\text{Raising}) $$
*   **Application**: Converting a "State Vector" (User) into a "Measurement Operator" (Test).
