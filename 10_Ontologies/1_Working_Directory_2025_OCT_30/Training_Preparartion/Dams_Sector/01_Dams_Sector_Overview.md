# Dams Sector - Comprehensive Overview

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-05
**Sector Classification:** Critical Infrastructure - Water Management & Hydroelectric Generation
**Regulatory Framework:** FERC, USACE, DHS, State Dam Safety Programs

## Executive Summary

The Dams Sector encompasses facilities that provide water retention and controlled water release services. This includes hydroelectric power generation, agricultural irrigation, municipal and industrial water supply, flood control, and recreational uses. The sector includes approximately 91,000 dams in the United States, with over 2,400 producing electricity and 8,100 classified as high-hazard potential.

## Sector Scope

### Primary Functions
- **Hydroelectric Power Generation**: Converting water flow energy into electrical power
- **Water Supply Management**: Municipal, industrial, and agricultural water distribution
- **Flood Control**: Managing water levels to prevent flooding
- **Navigation**: Maintaining waterway levels for commercial shipping
- **Recreation**: Supporting recreational activities and tourism
- **Irrigation**: Agricultural water supply and distribution

### Dam Classifications

**By Size:**
- Large Dams: Height ≥15 meters or volume ≥3 million cubic meters
- Small Dams: Height <15 meters
- Major Dams: Height ≥150 meters

**By Hazard Potential:**
- High Hazard: Loss of human life likely if failed
- Significant Hazard: Possible loss of human life, significant economic losses
- Low Hazard: No probable loss of human life, minimal economic losses

**By Type:**
- Gravity Dams: Concrete or masonry structures
- Arch Dams: Curved concrete structures
- Embankment Dams: Earth or rock-fill structures
- Buttress Dams: Supported by buttresses
- Roller-Compacted Concrete (RCC) Dams: Modern fast-construction type

## Critical Infrastructure Dependencies

### Upstream Dependencies
- **Electrical Grid**: Power supply for control systems and pumping
- **Communications**: SCADA, telemetry, emergency communications
- **Transportation**: Access roads for operations and emergency response
- **Weather Services**: Hydrological forecasting and monitoring

### Downstream Dependencies
- **Water Supply Systems**: Municipal and industrial water users
- **Agriculture**: Irrigation-dependent farming operations
- **Power Grid**: Hydroelectric generation capacity
- **Navigation**: Commercial shipping and transportation
- **Ecosystems**: Environmental flow requirements

## Sector Statistics

### United States Dam Inventory
- Total dams: ~91,000
- High-hazard potential: ~8,100
- Hydroelectric dams: ~2,400
- Federal dams: ~3,300
- Non-federal dams: ~87,700
- Average age: 61 years

### Hydroelectric Generation
- Total installed capacity: ~80,000 MW
- Annual generation: ~300 TWh (7% of U.S. electricity)
- Pumped storage capacity: ~22,000 MW
- Number of operating plants: ~1,450

### Economic Impact
- Infrastructure value: >$1 trillion
- Annual benefits: >$100 billion
- Employment: ~300,000 direct jobs
- Irrigated acreage: >10 million acres
