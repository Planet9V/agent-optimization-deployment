## 1. EMERGENCY SERVICES SECTOR

### Sector Overview
- **Sector Risk Management Agency (SRMA):** CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency)
- **Government Levels:** Federal, State, Local, Tribal, and Territorial
- **Private Sector Involvement:** Yes

### Official Subsectors (5 Total)

#### 1.1 LAW ENFORCEMENT
**Definition:** Responsible for enforcing laws, maintaining public order, and managing public safety, with primary duties including investigation, apprehension, and detention of individuals suspected of criminal offenses.

**Entity Count:** Over 18,000 agencies
- City police departments
- County sheriff's offices
- State law enforcement agencies
- Tribal police departments
- Federal law enforcement agencies
- University/college police departments

**Coordinating Agencies:**
- Department of Justice (DOJ)
- Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)
- CISA (Sector Risk Management)

#### 1.2 FIRE AND RESCUE SERVICES
**Definition:** Respond to natural disasters and man-made catastrophes, performing fire suppression, fire prevention, hazardous materials control, emergency rescue, building code enforcement, and public fire safety education.

**Entity Count:** Approximately 30,000+ fire departments
- Career fire departments
- Volunteer fire departments
- Combination career/volunteer departments
- Rural fire protection districts
- Industrial fire brigades

**Coordinating Agencies:**
- U.S. Fire Administration (USFA)
- Department of Homeland Security (DHS)
- CISA (Sector Risk Management)

#### 1.3 EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES (EMS)
**Definition:** A system providing pre-hospital emergency medical care for serious illness or injury and coordinated response and victim transport.

**Entity Count:** Over 18,200 local EMS agencies
- Licensed Personnel: More than 1.03 million (EMTs, paramedics, and other EMS patient care levels)
- Annual 911 Dispatches: Nearly 28.5 million (across 41 states)
- Coverage: All 50 states, District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, and American Samoa

**Coordinating Agencies:**
- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA)
- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA)
- CISA (Sector Risk Management)

#### 1.4 EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT
**Definition:** An essential government service whose purpose is to apply resources and efforts to mitigate, prevent, protect against, respond to, and recover from all threats and hazards.

**Entity Count:** All government levels
- Federal: FEMA, DHS
- State: State emergency management agencies (50 states + territories)
- Local: County and municipal emergency management agencies
- Tribal: Tribal emergency management offices

**Coordinating Agencies:**
- Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)
- Department of Homeland Security (DHS)
- CISA (Sector Risk Management)

#### 1.5 PUBLIC WORKS
**Definition:** Provides and sustains structures and services essential to public welfare, including water, power, waste disposal, and transportation.

**Entity Count:** Thousands of public works departments
- Municipal public works departments
- County public works agencies
- State departments of transportation
- Water and wastewater utilities
- Solid waste management facilities

**Coordinating Agencies:**
- Department of Transportation (DOT)
- Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
- CISA (Sector Risk Management)

## 2. FINANCIAL SERVICES SECTOR

### Sector Overview
- **Sector Risk Management Agency (SRMA):** U.S. Department of the Treasury
- **Total Entities:** 1,096,275 Finance and Insurance businesses (as of 2024)
- **Employment:** More than 6.7 million people (mid-2024)
- **Economic Impact:** 7.3% of U.S. GDP (2023)
- **Total Assets:** $23.7 trillion (banking system, end of 2023)

### Official Subsectors

#### 2.1 BANKING AND DEPOSITORY INSTITUTIONS
**Definition:** Institutions that accept deposits and provide credit intermediation services, including commercial banks, savings institutions, credit unions, and community banks.

**Entity Count (2023):**
- Total Banks: Approximately 4,200 banks with <$300M in deposits
- Large Banks: Over 1,801 banks with >$300M in deposits
- Historical Context: Consolidated from ~12,500 (1988) to current levels
- Credit Unions: Included in overall banking count

**Regulatory Agencies:**
- Federal Reserve System
- Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)
- Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC)
- National Credit Union Administration (NCUA)
- Department of the Treasury (SRMA)

**Services Provided:**
- Deposit accounts (checking, savings)
- Loans and credit products
- Payment processing
- Cash management
- Trust services

#### 2.2 SECURITIES AND INVESTMENT MANAGEMENT
**Definition:** Firms engaged in investment products, capital markets activities, securities trading, asset management, and private equity/venture capital.

**Entity Count:**
- Private Equity Firms: Nearly 5,000 investment firms
- PE-Backed Companies: 18,000 private equity-backed U.S. companies
- Venture Capital Firms: 3,417 VC firms (2023)
- Investment Management: Part of broader securities sector

**Regulatory Agencies:**
- Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
- Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA)
- Department of the Treasury (SRMA)

**Services Provided:**
- Securities brokerage and trading
- Investment advisory services
- Asset management
- Mergers and acquisitions
- Capital raising (IPOs, debt issuance)

#### 2.3 INSURANCE
**Definition:** Companies providing risk transfer products including life insurance, property and casualty insurance, health insurance, and reinsurance.

**Entity Count:**
- Insurance Establishments: 180,971 establishments (2022)
- Combined Direct Premiums: $1.9 trillion (2023)

**Types of Insurance Entities:**
- Life insurance companies
- Property and casualty insurers
- Health insurance providers
- Reinsurance companies
- Insurance brokers and agents

**Regulatory Agencies:**
- State Insurance Commissioners (primary regulation)
- Federal Insurance Office (FIO) at Treasury
- Department of the Treasury (SRMA)

**Services Provided:**
- Life and annuity products
- Property and casualty coverage
- Health insurance plans
- Reinsurance
- Risk management services

#### 2.4 PAYMENT, CLEARING, AND SETTLEMENT SYSTEMS
**Definition:** Critical financial utilities and services that process financial transactions, including payment networks, clearinghouses, and settlement systems.

**Entity Count:** Integrated across banking and financial services infrastructure
- Payment processors
- Automated Clearing House (ACH) networks
- Card networks (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover)
- Wire transfer systems (Fedwire, CHIPS)
- Securities clearinghouses

**Key Systems:**
- Fedwire Funds Service (Federal Reserve)
- Clearing House Interbank Payments System (CHIPS)
- Automated Clearing House (ACH) Network
- Securities settlement systems (DTCC)
- Card payment networks

**Regulatory Agencies:**
- Federal Reserve System
- Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)
- Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
- Department of the Treasury (SRMA)

**Services Provided:**
- Electronic funds transfers
- Check clearing and processing
- Securities clearing and settlement
- Card payment processing
- Cross-border payment services

### Financial Services Sector Coordinating Structure
- **Financial Services Sector Coordinating Council (FSSCC):** 24 sector associations and 46 financial institutions
- **Public-Private Partnership:** Strong collaboration between Treasury and FSSCC

## 3. FOOD AND AGRICULTURE SECTOR

### Sector Overview
- **Sector Risk Management Agencies (Co-SRMAs):**
  - U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)
  - U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
- **Ownership:** Almost entirely privately owned
- **Economic Impact:** 5.6% of U.S. GDP; 10.4% of U.S. employment
- **Direct Employment:** Over 24 million jobs (15% of U.S. employment)
- **Economic Activity:** More than $9.5 trillion

### Comprehensive Entity Count
- **Farms:** 1.9 million farms (estimated)
- **Restaurants:** Over 700,000 restaurants
- **Registered Food Facilities:** More than 220,000 (manufacturing, processing, storage)
- **Retail Food Stores:** Approximately 200,000
- **Total Restaurant/Foodservice:** More than 1 million locations

### Official Subsectors

#### 3.1 PRODUCTION AGRICULTURE
**Definition:** Direct farm operations involving the cultivation of crops, raising of livestock, and production of raw agricultural commodities including grains, fruits, vegetables, meat, poultry, dairy, and other farm products.

**Entity Count:**
- Total Farms and Ranches: Nearly 2 million farms
- Land Coverage: Two out of every five American acres
- Farm Types:
  - Crop production farms
  - Livestock and animal production farms
  - Mixed crop-livestock operations
  - Dairy farms
  - Poultry operations
  - Specialty crop farms (fruits, vegetables, nursery)

**Regulatory/Coordinating Agencies:**
- U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) - Co-SRMA
  - Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS)
  - Agricultural Marketing Service (AMS)
  - Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS)
  - Farm Service Agency (FSA)
- State Departments of Agriculture

**Economic Contribution:**
- Direct Farm Output: $222.3 billion (approximately 0.8% of U.S. GDP)

**Key Activities:**
- Crop cultivation (grains, oilseeds, fruits, vegetables)
- Livestock raising (cattle, hogs, poultry)
- Dairy production
- Aquaculture
- Forestry (related sector)

#### 3.2 FOOD MANUFACTURING AND PROCESSING
**Definition:** Establishments that transform raw food commodities into products for intermediate or final consumption by applying labor, machinery, energy, and scientific knowledge.

**Entity Count:**
- Food and Beverage Manufacturing Establishments: 42,708 establishments (2022)
- Food Manufacturing/Processing/Storage Facilities: Almost 200,000 facilities (registered with FDA)

**Employment:**
- Food and Beverage Manufacturing Workers: 1.7 million people (2021)
- Percentage of U.S. Employment: Just over 1.1%

**Major Processing Categories (by workforce %):**
1. Meat Processing: 30.6% of food/beverage manufacturing workers (2021)
2. Bakeries and Tortilla Producers: 14.7%
3. Other food and beverage processing: 54.7%

**Regulatory/Coordinating Agencies:**
- U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) - Co-SRMA
  - Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition (CFSAN)
  - Food safety inspection and regulation
- U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) - Co-SRMA
  - Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS) - for meat, poultry, egg products
- State and local health departments

**Regulatory Division (USDA vs FDA):**

**USDA Oversight:**
- Meat slaughtering and processing (beef, pork, lamb)
- Poultry processing (chicken, turkey)
- Processed egg products
- Ensures products are safe, wholesome, correctly labeled and packaged

**FDA Oversight:**
- Shell eggs
- Bottled water
- Wine beverages with <7% alcohol
- All other domestic and imported food sold in interstate commerce
- Food manufacturing facilities (excluding USDA-regulated products)

**Key Processing Activities:**
- Meat and poultry processing
- Dairy processing (cheese, milk, yogurt)
- Grain milling and baking
- Fruit and vegetable processing
- Beverage manufacturing
- Snack food production
- Prepared meals and convenience foods
- Frozen food manufacturing

#### 3.3 FOOD DISTRIBUTION AND RETAIL
**Definition:** Establishments involved in the storage, warehousing, distribution, wholesale, and retail sale of food products to consumers and businesses.

**Entity Count:**
- Retail Food Stores: Approximately 200,000
- Restaurants and Foodservice: More than 1 million locations
- Food and Beverage Stores: Part of broader food retail sector
- Warehousing and Storage: Included in 220,000+ registered facilities

**Types of Entities:**
- Grocery stores and supermarkets
- Convenience stores
- Specialty food stores
- Restaurants (full-service and quick-service)
- Institutional foodservice (schools, hospitals, prisons)
- Food wholesalers and distributors
- Cold storage facilities
- Food warehouses

**Regulatory/Coordinating Agencies:**
- FDA (food safety at retail level)
- USDA (for meat/poultry retail establishments)
- State and local health departments (retail food establishments)
- State agriculture departments

## KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

### Emergency Services Sector
- **Total Subsectors:** 5 (Law Enforcement, Fire/Rescue, EMS, Emergency Management, Public Works)
- **Approximate Total Entities:** 50,000+ agencies/departments
- **Workforce:** 1.03+ million licensed EMS personnel; thousands of law enforcement officers and firefighters
- **SRMA:** CISA
- **Critical Infrastructure Role:** First response to all emergencies and disasters

### Financial Services Sector
- **Total Subsectors:** 4 (Banking, Securities, Insurance, Payment Systems)
- **Total Entities:** 1,096,275+ finance and insurance businesses
- **Workforce:** 6.7+ million employees
- **Economic Impact:** 7.3% of U.S. GDP ($23.7 trillion in banking assets alone)
- **SRMA:** U.S. Department of the Treasury
- **Critical Infrastructure Role:** Economic stability and transaction processing

### Food and Agriculture Sector
- **Total Subsectors:** 3 (Production Agriculture, Food Manufacturing/Processing, Food Distribution/Retail)
- **Total Entities:**
  - 1.9 million farms
  - 220,000+ registered food facilities
  - 1+ million restaurants/foodservice
  - 200,000 retail food stores
- **Workforce:** 24+ million jobs (15% of U.S. employment)
- **Economic Impact:** 5.6% of U.S. GDP ($9.5+ trillion economic activity)
- **Co-SRMAs:** USDA and FDA
- **Critical Infrastructure Role:** Food security and nutrition for the nation
