## Executive Summary

Siemens Mobility is one of the world's leading providers of rail signaling and control systems, with comprehensive product portfolios spanning Communications-Based Train Control (CBTC), European Train Control System (ETCS), electronic interlocking, operations control, and automatic train protection systems. The company serves 56 operators across 5 continents in 25 countries and 49 cities, with 96 metro lines equipped and 4,351 trains serving more than 30 million passengers daily.

Siemens holds a strong competitive position in the smart railways market (20-25% combined share among top 5 players including Siemens, Alstom, Hitachi, Huawei, and Cisco). In 2024-2025, the company secured major framework contracts including a £900M UK Network Rail signaling framework and $754M HS2 high-speed rail contract.

### 2. ETCS (European Train Control System) Solutions

#### Overview

Siemens provides comprehensive ETCS Level 1 and Level 2 solutions for both trackside and onboard applications, compliant with ERTMS (European Rail Traffic Management System) specifications.

#### ETCS Level 2 - Trackside Infrastructure

**Core Components:**

**Radio Block Centre (RBC):**
- Retrieves signaling information from interlockings
- Converts data to ERTMS/ETCS compliant format
- Transmits movement authority via radio to trains
- Continuous supervision of train movements

**Eurobalises:**
- Passive positioning beacons ("electronic milestones")
- Provide precise location reference points
- No active power required

**Communication Systems:**
- GSM-R (Global System for Mobile Communications - Railway)
- FRMCS (Future Railway Mobile Communication System) - 5G based
- Continuous transmission of movement authority, speed limits, and route data

**Trainguard Radio Block Centre:**
- Central intelligence for ETCS Level 2 operations
- Interfaces with multiple interlocking systems
- Manages train movement authorities and track occupancy
- Enhanced cybersecurity features

#### ETCS Level 2 - Onboard Equipment

**European Vital Computer (EVC):**
- Safety-critical processing unit
- SIL 4 certified (Safety Integrity Level 4)
- Real-time train control calculations

**Driver Machine Interface (DMI):**
- In-cab display showing signaling information
- Speed supervision and target displays
- Alert and alarm management
- Replaces traditional lineside signals

**Communication Antennas:**
- GSM-R/FRMCS radio for RBC communication
- Eurobalise reader for positioning data

**Odometry System:**
- Precise train position determination
- Speed measurement and monitoring
- Integration with EVC for safe braking calculations

**Trainguard ETCS Onboard Units:**
- Modular, scalable design
- Compatible with multiple train types
- Retrofit and new-build applications

### 3. Trackguard Simis Electronic Interlocking Systems

Siemens' Simis family represents comprehensive electronic interlocking solutions for railway infrastructure control and safety management.

#### Simis Product Family

**Trackguard Simis W:**
- Modern, modular electronic interlocking
- Scalable from small stations to large terminal complexes
- Support for >1,000 outside elements in large installations

**Trackguard Simis IS (Interlocking Standard):**
- Standardized interlocking for individual customer requirements
- Flexible configuration options
- Cost-optimized deployment

**Simis S (Siemens Microcomputer Interlocking System S):**
- Proven technology platform
- Wide European deployment base

#### System Architecture

**Four-Level Architecture:**

1. **Control and Display Level:**
   - Human-machine interface
   - Graphical route management
   - Real-time status monitoring

2. **Central Interlocking Functions Level:**
   - Route logic processing
   - Safety rule enforcement
   - Conflict resolution

3. **Signaling and Safety Logic/Control and Monitoring Level:**
   - Vital processing units
   - Safety-critical decision making
   - Redundancy management

4. **Physical Level:**
   - Interface to field equipment
   - Points (switches), signals, track circuits
   - Detection systems

**Safety Architecture:**
- **2-out-of-3 redundant architecture**
- Vital computer systems (IIC/OMC and ACC)
- Hardware voting for safety-critical operations
- Independent channel processing with result verification

**Interlocking Principle:**
- Geographic-circuitry based
- Location and state-based infrastructure control
- Ensures safe train routing and conflict prevention

#### Hardware Platform

**SIMIS ECC Computer Cabinet:**
- Modular board-based architecture
- Hot-swappable components

**Board Types:**
- Central processing boards (vital CPU modules)
- Communication boards (network interfaces)
- Peripheral boards (field equipment interfaces)
- Power supply modules (redundant)

#### Communication

**Trackguard Simis DTS (Data Transmission System):**
- General interlocking information exchange
- Interface to operations control systems
- Remote diagnostics and monitoring capabilities

#### Scalability

Simis interlocking systems scale across wide range of applications:
- **Minimum:** Small branch stations with <10 elements
- **Medium:** Regional stations with 50-200 elements
- **Large:** Major terminals and junctions with 1,000+ elements

#### Safety Certification

- CENELEC EN 50126, EN 50128, EN 50129 compliant
- SIL 4 safety integrity level for interlocking functions
- Independent safety assessments and certifications
- Notified Body approvals across multiple jurisdictions

## Communication Protocols and Standards

### GSM-R (GSM-Railway)

**Overview:**
Siemens Mobility has been at the forefront of railway mobile communications for more than 25 years.

**Technical Specifications:**
- Based on GSM (Global System for Mobile Communications)
- 900 MHz frequency band
- Voice and data communication platform
- ETSI standardized for railway applications

**Key Features:**
- **Voice Group Call Service (VGCS):** Multi-party group calls for operational coordination
- **Voice Broadcast Service (VBS):** One-to-many emergency broadcasts
- **Location-Based Connections:** Automatic connectivity based on train position
- **Call Pre-emption:** Emergency call priority
- **GPRS/EGPRS Support:** Packet data for ETCS operation
- **IP-Based Protocols:** SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) integration

**Applications:**
- Driver-dispatcher communication
- Shunting team coordination
- Train engineer-to-station controller links
- Emergency communication
- ETCS data transmission

**Siemens Cab Radio Systems:**
- Robust and secure platform
- Worldwide deployments
- Integration with Siemens signaling portfolio

### FRMCS (Future Railway Mobile Communication System)

**Overview:**
5G-based successor to GSM-R, essential for modernization of railway operations.

**Technical Foundation:**
- Built on 5G technology
- MCX (Mission Critical Communication) standards (3GPP)
- 1,900 MHz frequency band allocation

**Enhanced Capabilities vs. GSM-R:**
- **Higher Throughput:** Increased data bandwidth for advanced applications
- **Lower Latency:** Critical for high-speed signaling and control
- **Video Integration:** Live video feeds for operations and security
- **Advanced Automation:** Support for GoA4 (fully driverless) operations
- **Flexible QoS:** Differentiated quality of service for multiple applications

**Siemens FRMCS Involvement:**

**Morane 2 Project:**
- Key stakeholder in FRMCS specification development
- Laboratory and field testing of all equipment
- Contribution to FRMCS specification version 3

**Digital S-Bahn Hamburg (2018):**
- Partnership with Deutsche Bahn, S-Bahn Hamburg, and Nokia
- Demonstration of fully automated shunting (GoA4) via 5G
- Proof-of-concept for FRMCS capabilities

**V4 Radio Technology:**
- Dual-band capability (900 MHz GSM-R + 1,900 MHz FRMCS)
- Modular 4G LTE hardware for future FRMCS adaptation
- Concurrent operation during migration period
- Software and hardware upgrade path

**Migration Strategy:**
- Backward compatibility with GSM-R
- Phased transition approach
- Minimize operational disruption
- Long-term coexistence during transition

### Additional Communication Technologies

**Airlink Radio Transmission Systems:**
- Proprietary Siemens radio technology
- Used in conjunction with Trainguard MT CBTC
- High-reliability wireless data links

**Ethernet-Based Communication:**
- Simis interlocking network interfaces
- Control center LAN/WAN connectivity
- Remote diagnostics and monitoring
