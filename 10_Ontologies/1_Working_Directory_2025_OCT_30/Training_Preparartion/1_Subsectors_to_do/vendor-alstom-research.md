## Executive Summary

Alstom is a world-leading provider of railway signaling and control systems with over 3,000 installations in more than 35 countries. The company holds a commanding 63% market share in ERTMS Level 2-compatible onboard equipment contracts and leads the global market for ETCS onboard equipment. In Europe, over 8 out of 10 trains operating under ETCS Level 2 utilize Alstom's Atlas solution, which has proven its reliability with over 1,100 trains covering more than 40 million kilometers of commercial service.

Alstom's comprehensive portfolio spans mainline ETCS/ERTMS systems, urban CBTC solutions, advanced interlocking systems, and integrated control center platforms. The company is actively driving the digital rail revolution with implementations across Europe, Australia, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East.

## 6. Communication Protocols and Standards

### 6.1 Current Standards

**GSM-R (GSM-Railway):**
- **Status:** Current European standard for railway communication
- **Application:** ETCS data transmission and critical voice communication
- **Deployment:** Alstom systems fully compatible with GSM-R networks
- **Limitations:** Technology approaching obsolescence

**GPRS (General Packet Radio Service):**
- **Application:** Data communication for non-critical applications
- **Integration:** Built into Alstom's onboard and trackside equipment

**GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System):**
- **Application:** Train positioning for ETCS Level 3 and ATO
- **Integration:** Onvia Cab includes GNSS receivers
- **Accuracy:** Enhanced positioning for safety-critical applications

**IP-Based Networking:**
- **Standard:** Ethernet/IP for interlocking and control systems
- **Security:** EN50159 compliant for safety-related communication
- **Flexibility:** Enables digital interlocking and remote control

### 6.2 Future Communication Systems

**FRMCS (Future Railway Mobile Communication System):**
- **Status:** Next-generation railway communication standard
- **Timeline:** Progressive deployment replacing GSM-R
- **Alstom Readiness:** Current Onvia Cab and Control systems FRMCS-ready
- **Benefits:** Higher bandwidth, lower latency, enhanced security
- **Technology Base:** 5G-based railway-specific communication

**LTE-R (Long-Term Evolution for Railways):**
- **Status:** Transition technology between GSM-R and FRMCS
- **Application:** High-speed data for ATO, video, and passenger services
- **Alstom Support:** 4G-5G integration in current products

### 6.3 Safety and Security Standards

**EN50159:**
- **Purpose:** Safety-related communication in railway applications
- **Compliance:** All Alstom signaling products EN50159 certified
- **Scope:** Data integrity, authentication, and protection against threats

**Cybersecurity Framework:**
- **Built-In Security:** Integrated into Onvia Lock and Onvia Control
- **Standards Compliance:** IEC 62443 (industrial cybersecurity)
- **Threat Protection:** Defense against common cyber attack vectors
- **Secure Communication:** Encrypted data transmission
- **Access Control:** Role-based authentication and authorization

**ISO 55001 (Asset Management):**
- **Certification:** Alstom Netherlands holds ISO 55001 for railway security
- **Application:** Comprehensive asset lifecycle management
- **Benefits:** Optimized maintenance and extended equipment life
