# DET-TRONICS PointWatch Eclipse Infrared CO2 Gas Detector: Comprehensive Security Analysis

## Abstract

The PointWatch Eclipse Infrared CO2 Gas Detector represents DET-TRONICS' specialized infrared gas detection technology for carbon dioxide monitoring in enhanced oil recovery sites and CO2 suppression cylinder storage applications. This security analysis examines the technical architecture, threat landscape, and deployment security considerations of CO2 detection systems across critical infrastructure applications. The investigation reveals significant security implications arising from the detector's specialized application requirements, communication interfaces, and integration architecture, particularly concerning potential manipulation of enhanced oil recovery process operations and fire suppression system functionality.

## Introduction

The PointWatch Eclipse Infrared CO2 Gas Detector constitutes a specialized component within DET-TRONICS' gas detection portfolio, utilizing non-dispersive infrared spectroscopy technology to provide enhanced oil recovery site monitoring and CO2 suppression cylinder storage applications (DET-TRONICS, 2025a). ## Technical Architecture Analysis

### Non-Dispersive Infrared CO2 Detection Technology

The CO2 detector employs sophisticated non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) spectroscopy technology specifically designed for carbon dioxide detection in enhanced oil recovery and fire suppression applications (DET-TRONICS, 2025b). The detector's electronic system analyzes infrared radiation absorption characteristics at wavelengths specific to carbon dioxide detection, providing reliable CO2 concentration monitoring through factory-calibrated detection algorithms. This specialized detection approach, while operationally beneficial for targeted applications, creates potential attack vectors through application-specific algorithmic manipulation or targeted signal interference techniques.

The diffusion-based point-type detector design enables standalone deployment or integration with total facility safety systems, providing flexible installation options for diverse industrial environments (DET-TRONICS, 2025c). The detector's robust and reliable construction ensures operation in harsh environments typical of enhanced oil recovery sites and CO2 storage facilities, with non-intrusive calibration capabilities enabling maintenance activities without system shutdown requirements. This sophisticated detection capability, while providing operational advantages for specialized applications, introduces computational vulnerabilities potentially exploitable through sophisticated input manipulation or environmental interference techniques.

### Communication Interface Architecture

The CO2 detector incorporates multiple communication interfaces enabling integration with diverse industrial control systems and safety networks specific to enhanced oil recovery and fire suppression applications. The system supports 4-20 mA analog current loop signaling for compatibility with traditional industrial systems, Modbus RTU communication for SCADA integration, relay contact outputs for discrete alarm signaling, and HART protocol communication for process information exchange (DET-TRONICS, 2025f). While these communication options facilitate flexible deployment scenarios, they simultaneously create substantial attack surface exposure through multiple potential compromise vectors and protocol-specific security limitations.

The integration compatibility with DET-TRONICS' broader safety system architecture includes compatibility with FlexVu display units and Eagle Quantum Premier systems through appropriate interface configurations (DET-TRONICS, 2025g). The system compatibility approach, while operationally flexible, introduces potential security considerations through compatibility requirements with legacy industrial protocols and enhanced oil recovery process control systems exhibiting known security vulnerabilities.
