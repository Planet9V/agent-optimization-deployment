## LIMITATIONS & CONSIDERATIONS

### When Traditional Red Teams May Be Preferred

**1. High-Touch Client Engagement Requirements**
- Some organizations prefer extensive human interaction and collaborative planning
- Traditional teams provide more "hand-holding" through the process
- Agent Zero is more autonomous, requiring less client time but offering less collaborative exploration

**2. Highly Customized Attack Scenarios**
- Novel attack scenarios requiring creative human problem-solving beyond documented TTPs
- Agent Zero excels at known adversary behaviors but may be less creative in entirely novel scenarios
- Traditional teams can "think outside the box" in ways AI may not initially consider

**3. Physical Security Testing (Complex)**
- While Agent Zero handles digital social engineering well, complex physical security testing (e.g., facility infiltration with improvisation) may benefit from human adaptability
- Agent Zero can plan physical attacks but execution requires human operatives (can be coordinated)

**4. Regulatory or Compliance Mandates**
- Some regulatory frameworks explicitly require "manual penetration testing" by human teams
- Agent Zero may not satisfy certain compliance checkbox requirements (though this is changing)

**5. Organizational Culture Fit**
- Risk-averse organizations may be uncomfortable with AI-driven offensive operations
- Some security teams prefer learning directly from human red team members during purple team sessions

### Agent Zero Limitations (Transparently Disclosed)

**1. Novel Zero-Day Exploitation**
- Agent Zero uses documented exploits and techniques from knowledge graph
- While it can discover new vulnerability instances, novel zero-day exploit development requires human expertise
- **Mitigation:** Agent Zero partners with human exploit developers for advanced scenarios

**2. Complex Social Engineering Nuance**
- Agent Zero handles email/phone social engineering well but may lack human intuition for real-time complex interpersonal dynamics
- **Mitigation:** Human social engineers available for high-complexity pretexting scenarios

**3. Highly Air-Gapped Environments**
- Agent Zero requires network connectivity for autonomous operation
- True air-gapped networks (no internet, no internal connectivity) limit Agent Zero's effectiveness
- **Mitigation:** Hybrid approach with Agent Zero planning and human execution in air-gapped segments

**4. Legal and Ethical Gray Areas**
- Agent Zero operates within pre-defined rules of engagement but cannot make nuanced ethical judgments that humans can
- If unexpected ethical dilemmas arise, human oversight required
- **Mitigation:** 24/7 human oversight team monitors operations with emergency stop authority
