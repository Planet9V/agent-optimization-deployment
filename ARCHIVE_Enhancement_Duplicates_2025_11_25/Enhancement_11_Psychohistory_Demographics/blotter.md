## ISSUE TRACKER

### Issue 001: The Mule Problem (Open)
**Priority:** MEDIUM
**Category:** Theoretical Limitation
**Description:** Asimov's psychohistory fails for unpredictable individuals (The Mule). Cyber analog: zero-day exploits, insider threats, novel threat actors.

**Impact:**
- Model cannot predict zero-day discovery timing
- Individual insider threat psychology not covered
- Breakthrough offensive techniques (e.g., Stuxnet) are "Mule events"

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Document model limitations in all outputs
- Combine with Enhancement 10 (Psychometric Individual Analysis) for comprehensive coverage
- Use psychohistory for campaign-level predictions, not individual attack forecasting
- Implement uncertainty flags for high-Mule-risk scenarios

**Status:** Acknowledged, mitigation planned

---

### Issue 002: Ethical Concerns - Demographic Profiling (Open)
**Priority:** HIGH
**Category:** Ethical Risk
**Description:** Population-level modeling could enable discriminatory targeting or manipulation if misused.

**Concerns:**
- Demographic stereotyping ("All Boomers are vulnerable")
- Weaponization for offensive operations (mass manipulation campaigns)
- Privacy violations (population tracking without consent)
- Cultural insensitivity (Western-centric norms)

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Defensive use only (enhance awareness, not offensive operations)
- Ethical review board oversight for all deployments
- Avoid deterministic labeling (use probabilistic language: "68% likelihood")
- Cultural sensitivity audits by diverse consultation teams
- Transparent documentation of model assumptions and biases

**Status:** Mitigation plan implemented, ongoing monitoring required

---

### Issue 003: Training Data Bias (Open)
**Priority:** MEDIUM
**Category:** Data Quality
**Description:** Training data may reflect Western-centric perspectives, outdated cultural norms, or demographic assumptions.

**Potential Biases:**
- US/European focus (underrepresentation of Asia, Africa, Latin America)
- 2020-2024 temporal window (pandemic-era behaviors may not generalize)
- English language sources (linguistic and cultural bias)
- Academic/technical author perspectives (not representative of general population)

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Cross-reference with global data sources (World Bank, OECD, Pew Research)
- Regular updates as cultural norms evolve (annual refresh cycle)
- Flag high-uncertainty predictions with confidence intervals
- Seek diverse cultural consultation for global deployments
- Explicitly document data provenance and limitations

**Status:** Acknowledged, cross-referencing planned for Phase 4

---

### Issue 004: Computational Complexity (Open)
**Priority:** LOW
**Category:** Performance
**Description:** Population-level queries may be slow on large graphs (millions of nodes, billions of relationships).

**Performance Concerns:**
- Complex Cypher queries with multiple MATCH clauses
- Aggregations over large population cohorts (e.g., "all US Millennials")
- Real-time prediction calculations (Bayesian inference)
- Dashboard refresh latency for analysts

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Neo4j index optimization (age_range, sector, region attributes)
- Query result caching for common demographic patterns
- Pre-computed aggregations for dashboard views
- Parallel query execution using Neo4j clustering (if needed)
- Incremental model updates (not full graph recalculation)

**Status:** Performance testing scheduled for Phase 3, optimization deferred until bottlenecks identified
