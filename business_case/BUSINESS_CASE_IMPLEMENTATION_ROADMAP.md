## Implementation Roadmap Overview

The WAVE 3 go-to-market strategy is structured in four phases across a 36-month timeline, moving from product validation through market leadership establishment. This roadmap details resource allocation, customer acquisition, product development, and market positioning.

**Key Success Factors:**
- **Execution velocity**: Launch within 4 months to establish category leadership
- **Customer validation**: Achieve 3 reference customers by Month 14
- **Market positioning**: Establish thought leadership and analyst recognition
- **Ecosystem partnerships**: Partner with top consulting firms for distribution
- **Team retention**: Maintain core domain expertise throughout execution

## Risk Management & Contingency Plans

### Critical Risks & Mitigation

**Risk 1: Slower-than-expected customer acquisition**
- Trigger: <3 customers closed per month by Month 9
- Impact: Revenue targets miss, profitability delayed
- Mitigation:
  - Accelerate partner channel (existing relationships to close faster)
  - Increase sales team to drive higher activity
  - Offer pilot programs to reduce customer risk/commitment

**Risk 2: Product integration challenges**
- Trigger: >2 customer deployments with >4-week delays beyond timeline
- Impact: Customer dissatisfaction, references hurt, NPS declines
- Mitigation:
  - Allocate engineering resources to customer integrations
  - Standardize integration patterns (reduce custom engineering)
  - Partner with systems integrators for complex deployments

**Risk 3: Competitive response from incumbents**
- Trigger: Splunk, Palo Alto Networks, or Microsoft announce energy security product
- Impact: Sales cycle extension, pricing pressure, feature parity required
- Mitigation:
  - Accelerate customer acquisition (lock-in before competition launches)
  - Deepen customer relationships (switching costs increase)
  - Establish analyst relationships (thought leadership positioning)
  - Continue product innovation (stay ahead of competitor offerings)

**Risk 4: Regulatory environment change**
- Trigger: NERC CIP standards significantly revised or eliminated
- Impact: Product assumptions invalidated, customer value reduced
- Mitigation:
  - Maintain NERC relationship (early notification of standard changes)
  - Build flexibility into product (standards become pluggable modules)
  - Expand to adjacent regulatory frameworks (EU NIS2, IEC 62443)

**Risk 5: Talent retention (domain experts)**
- Trigger: >1 of 3 core NERC CIP experts departs
- Impact: Product quality suffers, regulatory expertise gap, customer relationships at risk
- Mitigation:
  - Retention bonuses (Year 1-2 cliff, vesting at Month 18)
  - Equity incentives (long-term wealth creation)
  - Career development (clear advancement pathways)
  - Competitive compensation (market-rate salaries + benefits)

### Decision Gates & Pivot Triggers

**Month 6 Gate (End of Phase 1):**
- **Continue if**: >80% success metrics achieved, 5+ new customer commitments, analyst recognition
- **Pivot if**: <80% metrics, <3 commitments (reassess positioning/value prop)
- **Kill if**: >3 critical bugs, analyst feedback negative, customer dissatisfaction (NPS <40)

**Month 12 Gate (Q4 Year 1):**
- **Continue if**: 25+ customers, $2.1M ARR, 40%+ gross margin, $1.2M+ pipeline

- **Reassess if**: Competitive response faster than expected, requires acceleration

**Month 18 Gate (End of Phase 2, Profitability Path):**
- **Continue if**: 50+ customers, $4.2M ARR, break-even achieved, strong pipeline
- **Reassess if**: Revenue target miss >20%, customer churn >10%, market saturation evident
