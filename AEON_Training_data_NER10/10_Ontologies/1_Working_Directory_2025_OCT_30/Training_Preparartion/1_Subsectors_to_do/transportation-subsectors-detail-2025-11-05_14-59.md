# Transportation Sector Subsectors - Complete Breakdown

**Research Date:** 2025-11-05
**Timestamp:** 14:59
**Sector:** Transportation Systems
**Source Authority:** DHS/CISA Critical Infrastructure

---

## Executive Summary

The Transportation Systems Sector comprises **seven (7) primary modes/subsectors** with extensive infrastructure components, facility types, and operational categories. This document provides the complete detailed breakdown of each subsector.

**Total Primary Subsectors: 7**

---

## Transportation Modes (High-Level Subsectors)

1. Aviation
2. Highway and Motor Carrier
3. Maritime Transportation System
4. Mass Transit and Passenger Rail
5. Pipeline Systems
6. Freight Rail
7. Postal and Shipping

## Detailed Breakdown by Subsector

### 1. AVIATION SUBSECTOR

**Overview:**
Aviation includes aircraft, air traffic control systems, and approximately 19,700 airports, heliports, and landing strips, with about 500 providing commercial aviation services.

**Facility Types:**

#### Airport Categories (FAA Classification)
1. **Commercial Service Airports**
   - Primary commercial airports
   - Civil airports
   - Joint-use military airports
   - Approximately 500 facilities

2. **Cargo Service Airports**
   - Aircraft providing air transportation of cargo only
   - Total annual landed weight > 100 million pounds
   - Dedicated cargo terminals
   - Air freight facilities

3. **General Aviation Airports**
   - Public-use airports without scheduled service
   - Less than 2,500 annual passenger boardings
   - Private aviation facilities
   - Approximately 19,200+ facilities

4. **Reliever Airports**
   - Designated by FAA to relieve congestion at commercial airports
   - Improved general aviation access
   - Regional distribution function

#### Facility Types and Infrastructure Components
- **Runways and taxiways**
- **Control towers**
- **Firefighting and rescue services**
- **Passenger terminals**
- **Cargo facilities**
- **General aviation facilities**
- **Parking garages**
- **Access road networks**
- **Public transport connections**
- **Lighting systems**
- **Navigational and approach aids**
- **Aircraft repair stations**
- **Fueling facilities**
- **Flight schools**
- **Heliports**
- **Seaplane bases**

#### Aircraft Categories
1. **Commercial Aircraft**
   - Passenger airliners
   - Cargo aircraft
   - Charter services

2. **Recreational Aircraft**
   - Private aviation
   - Sport aircraft

3. **Unmanned Aircraft**
   - Manned aircraft systems
   - Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs)
   - Drone operations

**Sub-Category Count: ~15-20 distinct facility/operation types**

3. **Polyfunctional Ports**

4. **Inland Ports**

6. **Dry Ports**

6. **Inland Waterway Vessels**

### 6. FREIGHT RAIL SUBSECTOR

**Overview:**
Consists of seven major carriers, hundreds of smaller railroads, over 138,000 miles of active railroad, over 1.33 million freight cars, and approximately 20,000 locomotives, with an estimated 12,000 trains operating daily.

**Facility Types:**

#### Railroad Yard Types
1. **Classification Yards** (Marshalling Yards)
   - Freight sorting and classification
   - Multi-directional freight intake
   - Largest: Bailey Yard (Union Pacific) - 10,000 cars/day
   - National distribution hubs

2. **Receiving Yards** (Arrival Yards)
   - Freight car detachment from locomotives
   - Mechanical problem inspection
   - Initial processing

3. **Departure Yards**
   - Car block assembly
   - Train formation
   - Outbound preparation

4. **Car Repair Yards** (Maintenance Yards)
   - Freight car maintenance
   - Inspection facilities
   - Repair operations

5. **Transfer Yards**
   - Consist drop-off/pickup
   - Through-service operations
   - Unit train assembly

#### Yard Design Types
1. **Flat Yards**
   - No hump design
   - Locomotive-based car movement
   - All switching by engines

2. **Hump Yards**
   - Constructed hill design
   - Gravity-assisted sorting
   - Automated classification
   - High-volume operations

3. **Gravity Yards**
   - Natural slope design
   - Reduced locomotive dependence
   - Terrain-based operations

#### Terminal Types
1. **Intermodal Terminals**
   - Container transfer (train-to-truck)
   - Cranes and lifting equipment
   - Straddle carriers
   - Modern tracking systems
   - Fast, efficient transfers

2. **Bulk Freight Terminals**
   - Commodity-specific facilities
   - Loading terminals
   - Unloading terminals
   - Dedicated equipment

3. **General Freight Terminals**
   - Mixed cargo handling
   - Break-bulk operations
   - Distribution centers

#### Supporting Infrastructure
1. **Engine Terminals**
   - Light locomotive maintenance
   - Sanding towers
   - Wash racks
   - Fueling pads
   - Daily servicing

2. **Heavy Maintenance Facilities**
   - Major locomotive overhaul
   - Heavy repairs
   - Component replacement

3. **Shops**
   - Locomotive inspection
   - Railcar inspection
   - Maintenance operations
   - Repair facilities
   - Overhaul operations

4. **Switch Yards**
   - Track switching operations
   - Routing control
   - Train assembly

5. **Locks** (Canal/Waterway Integration)
   - Water-rail transfer
   - Elevation management

6. **Maintenance Bases**
   - Regional maintenance
   - Equipment storage
   - Supply depots

7. **Operation Control Centers**
   - Traffic management
   - Dispatch operations
   - Safety monitoring
   - Communications hub

#### Rolling Stock
- **1.33+ million freight cars**
- **20,000 locomotives**
- **12,000 trains operating daily**

#### Track Infrastructure
- **138,000+ miles of active railroad**
- **Seven major carriers** (Class I railroads)
- **Hundreds of smaller railroads** (Regional and shortline)

**Sub-Category Count: ~20-25 distinct facility/yard/terminal types**
