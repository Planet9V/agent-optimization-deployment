# Healthcare and Public Health Sector Subsectors - Complete Breakdown

**Research Date:** 2025-11-05 14:59
**Sector:** Healthcare and Public Health (HPH) Critical Infrastructure Sector
**Sector Risk Management Agency:** U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
**Division:** Administration for Strategic Preparedness and Response (ASPR)

---

## Executive Summary

The Healthcare and Public Health (HPH) Sector is one of 16 critical infrastructure sectors in the United States. The sector's critical infrastructure is classified according to service types and functional categories, resulting in **eight (8) distinct subsectors**: six (6) private sector subsectors and two (2) government subsectors.

**Total Healthcare Subsectors: 8**

---

## High-Level Subsector Classification

### Six Private Sector Subsectors:
1. Direct Patient Healthcare
2. Health Information Technology (Health IT)
3. Health Plans and Payers
4. Pharmaceuticals, Labs, and Blood
5. Medical Materials / Medical Technology
6. Mass Fatality Management Services

### Two Government Subsectors:
7. Federal Program Offices
8. Public Health Agencies (Federal, State, Local, Tribal, and Territorial)

---

## Detailed Subsector Breakdown

### 1. DIRECT PATIENT HEALTHCARE (DIRECT PATIENT CARE)

**Description:** The largest subsector encompassing all hands-on patient care facilities and services.

**Sub-categories:**
- General and surgical hospitals
- Psychiatric hospitals
- Ambulatory healthcare facilities
- Ambulatory care centers
- Extended care facilities (nursing homes, skilled nursing facilities)
- Long-term care centers
- Dialysis centers
- Individual practitioner offices
- Medical clinics
- Outpatient care centers
- Same-day surgical centers
- Emergency medical services (EMS)
- Urgent care centers

**Key Characteristics:**
- Majority of Healthcare Sector Coordinating Council (HSCC) partners
- Primarily privately owned and operated
- Direct patient interaction and treatment

---

### 2. HEALTH INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (HEALTH IT)

**Description:** Encompasses all partners that work to secure networks or provide tools for sharing health information across the sector.

**Sub-categories:**
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems
- Health Information Exchanges (HIE)
- Healthcare network security providers
- Medical data analytics platforms
- Telemedicine/telehealth platforms
- Healthcare cybersecurity services
- Clinical information systems
- Medical device software systems
- Health data interoperability tools

**Key Characteristics:**
- Critical for information sharing
- Cybersecurity-focused
- Enables digital healthcare delivery

---

### 3. HEALTH PLANS AND PAYERS

**Description:** Major insurance organizations and payment systems supporting healthcare financing.

**Sub-categories:**
- Private health insurance companies
- Managed care organizations (MCOs)
- Medicare Advantage plans
- Medicaid managed care organizations
- Health maintenance organizations (HMOs)
- Preferred provider organizations (PPOs)
- Third-party administrators (TPAs)
- Pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs)
- Self-insured employer health plans

**Key Characteristics:**
- Healthcare financing infrastructure
- Major insurance plans representation
- Payment processing and claims management

---

### 4. PHARMACEUTICALS, LABS, AND BLOOD

**Description:** Critical subsector for medical testing, pharmaceutical supply, and blood availability during disasters and responses.

**Sub-categories:**

**Pharmaceuticals:**
- Pharmaceutical manufacturing operations
- Retail pharmacies
- Hospital pharmacies
- Compounding pharmacies
- Drug distribution networks
- Pharmaceutical wholesale distributors

**Laboratories:**
- Clinical diagnostic laboratories
- Medical testing laboratories
- Reference laboratories
- Public health laboratories
- Research laboratories
- Pathology laboratories

**Blood Services:**
- Blood banks
- Blood collection centers
- Tissue banks
- Organ procurement organizations
- Plasma collection centers

**Key Characteristics:**
- Essential for emergency response
- Time-sensitive materials
- Highly regulated operations

---

### 5. MEDICAL MATERIALS / MEDICAL TECHNOLOGY

**Description:** Encompasses pharmaceutical supplies, medical-surgical supplies, medical devices, and healthcare equipment.

**Sub-categories:**
- Medical device manufacturers
- Medical equipment manufacturers
- Surgical supply manufacturers
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) manufacturers
- Medical-surgical (med-surge) supplies
- Durable medical equipment (DME)
- Diagnostic equipment manufacturers
- Biomedical technology companies
- Prosthetics and orthotics manufacturers
- Medical imaging equipment
- Biomaterials producers

**Key Characteristics:**
- Supply chain critical
- Manufacturing and distribution
- Equipment and technology focus

---

### 6. MASS FATALITY MANAGEMENT SERVICES

**Description:** Services for handling mass casualty events and fatality management during disasters.

**Sub-categories:**
- Funeral homes
- Funeral director associations
- Mortuaries
- Crematoriums
- Cemeteries
- Coroner offices (private contractors)
- Body transport services
- Mass casualty incident (MCI) response teams

**Key Characteristics:**
- Disaster and emergency response focused
- Community-based services
- Essential for public health emergencies

---

### 7. FEDERAL PROGRAM OFFICES (GOVERNMENT SUBSECTOR)

**Description:** Federal-level healthcare programs, services, and administrative offices.

**Sub-categories:**
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
- Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
- Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA)
- Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA)
- Administration for Strategic Preparedness and Response (ASPR)
- Indian Health Service (IHS)
- Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ)
- National Institutes of Health (NIH) healthcare programs
- Veterans Health Administration (VHA)
- Department of Defense (DoD) healthcare facilities
- Federal Bureau of Prisons healthcare services

**Key Characteristics:**
- Federal oversight and regulation
- Program administration
- Federal healthcare delivery systems

---

### 8. PUBLIC HEALTH AGENCIES (GOVERNMENT SUBSECTOR)

**Description:** Government public health infrastructure at all levels of government.

**Sub-categories:**

**Federal Level:**
- CDC programs and field offices
- HHS regional offices
- Federal public health emergency response
- National public health initiatives

**State Level:**
- State health departments
- State public health laboratories
- State epidemiology programs
- State emergency preparedness offices

**Local Level:**
- County health departments
- City health departments
- Local public health clinics
- Community health centers

**Tribal Level:**
- Tribal health departments
- Tribal epidemiology centers
- Tribal public health services

**Territorial Level:**
- Puerto Rico Department of Health
- U.S. Virgin Islands Department of Health
- Guam Department of Public Health and Social Services
- American Samoa Department of Health
- Northern Mariana Islands Department of Public Health

**Key Characteristics:**
- Government-operated
- Population health focus
- Emergency preparedness and response
- Regulatory and oversight functions

## Sector Coordination Structure

**Healthcare Sector Coordinating Council (HSCC):**
The private sector side of the partnership, with sub-councils representing each of the six private subsectors:
1. Direct Patient Care Sub-council
2. Health IT Sub-council
3. Health Plans and Payers Sub-council
4. Pharmaceuticals, Labs, and Blood Sub-council
5. Medical Materials/Medical Technology Sub-council
6. Mass Fatality Management Services Sub-council

**Government Coordinating Council (GCC):**
The government side representing federal, state, local, tribal, and territorial public health and federal program offices.

**Partnership Coordination:**
- Co-chaired by CISA and HHS/ASPR
- Public-private collaboration model
- Critical infrastructure protection focus
- Cybersecurity and resilience enhancement

---

## Key Sector Characteristics

**Ownership Structure:**
- Vast majority privately owned and operated (approximately 85% private)
- Significant government presence in public health infrastructure
- Mix of for-profit and non-profit entities

**Critical Infrastructure Elements:**
- Assets, systems, and networks vital to national security
- Essential for public health and safety
- Economic security importance
- Protection from terrorism, pandemics, natural disasters, and cyber threats

**Interdependencies:**
- Highly interconnected subsectors
- Dependencies on other critical infrastructure sectors (Energy, Water, Communications, IT)
- Supply chain vulnerabilities across subsectors

---

## Sector Scale and Scope

**Economic Impact:**
- Represents approximately 18% of U.S. GDP
- Employs millions of healthcare workers
- Extensive supply chain networks

**Geographic Distribution:**
- Nationwide presence across all 50 states
- Urban and rural healthcare facilities
- Tribal and territorial coverage

**Service Diversity:**
- Prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and recovery services
- Public health surveillance and response
- Research and development
- Healthcare education and training
- Emergency medical services
