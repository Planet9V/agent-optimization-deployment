## MDT Software and Operating Systems

### Windows 10/11 IoT Enterprise
- **OS**: Windows 10 IoT Enterprise LTSC (Long-Term Servicing Channel)
- **Purpose**: Specialized Windows for embedded/mobile devices
- **Support Lifecycle**: 10-year support lifecycle (vs 18-30 months for consumer Windows)
- **Updates**: Security updates only (no feature updates during lifecycle)
- **Lockdown**: Unified Write Filter (UWF) for disk protection
- **Customization**: Custom Start menu, app whitelisting
- **Management**: SCCM, Intune, Group Policy management
- **Licensing**: OEM licensing for device manufacturers
- **Price**: $100-$300 per device license

### Android Enterprise (Android for Public Safety)
- **OS**: Android 10/11/12/13 Enterprise Edition
- **Purpose**: Managed Android for fleet devices
- **MDM**: Mobile Device Management (VMware Workspace ONE, MobileIron, Samsung Knox)
- **Kiosk Mode**: Lock device to single app or restricted apps
- **Security**: Verified Boot, hardware-backed keystore, SELinux
- **Updates**: Regular security patches (monthly)
- **App Distribution**: Private enterprise app store
- **BYOD**: Support for work profile on personal devices
- **Licensing**: Free (included with Android), MDM licensing separate

## MDT Network Connectivity

### 4G LTE Mobile Broadband
- **Technology**: 4G LTE (Long-Term Evolution)
- **Carriers**: AT&T FirstNet, Verizon Frontline, T-Mobile
- **Frequency Bands**: Band 14 (FirstNet dedicated 700 MHz), Band 13 (Verizon), Band 12 (T-Mobile)
- **Download Speed**: 10-50 Mbps typical, 100+ Mbps peak
- **Upload Speed**: 5-20 Mbps typical
- **Latency**: 30-50ms
- **Modems**: Sierra Wireless EM7455 (CAT6), Qualcomm X20 (CAT16), X55 (CAT22)
- **Carrier Aggregation**: Combine multiple LTE bands for higher speed
- **Priority Access**: Public safety priority and preemption on FirstNet/Frontline

### 5G Mobile Broadband (Emerging)
- **Technology**: 5G NR (New Radio)
- **Frequency Bands**: Sub-6 GHz (n71, n77, n78), mmWave (n260, n261)
- **Download Speed**: 100-500 Mbps typical, 1+ Gbps peak
- **Upload Speed**: 50-200 Mbps
- **Latency**: 10-20ms (ultra-low latency)
- **Use Case**: High-bandwidth applications (video streaming, real-time video)
- **Availability**: Urban areas first, expanding to rural
- **Modems**: Qualcomm X55/X60/X65 5G modems
