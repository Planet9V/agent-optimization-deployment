# Preventive Maintenance Procedures

## Transformer Maintenance Programs

### Power Transformer Inspection Schedule
**Quarterly Inspection Protocol**: Maintenance_Technician performs visual inspection of Transformer_Unit_T-445 (Siemens 400 MVA, 345/138 kV, ONAN/ONAF cooling) checking Oil_Level_Indicator reading 25°C reference mark on Qualitrol-117 magnetic gauge and Nitrogen_Pressure_Blanket maintaining 3 psig measured by Ashcroft pressure gauge. The inspection includes Bushing_Condition_Assessment examining three ABB-GOB-420 oil-filled bushings (420 kV BIL rating) for oil leaks, cracked porcelain, or contamination, and Radiator_Bank inspection verifying cooling fan operation on four Siemens cooling modules (forced-air rating 133 MVA per stage). Technician_Lead documents findings in CMMS-Maximo work order WO-2024-T445-Q3 noting bushing power factor test due based on 3-year test cycle per manufacturer recommendations.

**Annual Oil Analysis**: Laboratory_Technician collects oil sample from Transformer_Unit_T-445 sample valve using Doble-S32 portable oil sampler (vacuum extraction method preventing atmospheric contamination) shipping sample to Testing_Laboratory for dissolved gas analysis per ASTM-D3612 standard. The Morgan-Schaffer-Calisto DGA analyzer measures key gases: Hydrogen 89 ppm (normal <100 ppm), Methane 23 ppm (normal <50 ppm), Ethane 12 ppm (normal <50 ppm), Ethylene 8 ppm (normal <50 ppm), Acetylene 1 ppm (normal <3 ppm indicating no arcing), Carbon-Monoxide 345 ppm, Carbon-Dioxide 3,456 ppm with CO/CO2 ratio 0.10 (normal <0.3) indicating normal cellulose aging. Test results show Total_Dissolved_Combustible_Gas 133 ppm (normal condition per IEEE-C57.104 interpretation), no action required, next sampling interval 12 months.

### Transformer Testing Procedures
**Power Factor Testing**: Test_Engineer performs Doble-M4000 power factor test on Transformer_Unit_T-667 (Siemens 225 MVA, 345/138 kV) measuring insulation power factor 0.48% at 10 kV test voltage (normal <1.0% for oil-paper insulation at 20°C). The test configuration includes CH capacitance 9,450 pF and CL capacitance 8,890 pF on high-voltage winding measuring insulation resistance 12,500 MegaOhms at 10 kV sustained for 10-minute duration. Engineer_Analysis compares current test results to baseline measurements from commissioning showing power factor increase from 0.35% to 0.48% over 15-year service period indicating gradual normal insulation aging, no immediate action required but monitoring continues with annual test frequency.

**Turns Ratio Test**: Using Vanguard-TTR-35A transformer turns ratio tester, Test_Technician measures Transformer_Unit_T-556 (ABB 300 MVA, 345/138 kV with 33-position tapchanger) turns ratio across all tap positions validating nameplate ratio 2.500:1 (345kV/138kV). The test results show Tap_16_Neutral measured ratio 2.5018:1 (0.072% deviation from calculated, within ±0.5% acceptance), Tap_1_Maximum_Buck 2.7523:1 (10% buck position, 0.084% deviation), and Tap_33_Maximum_Boost 2.2727:1 (10% boost position, 0.099% deviation) all within IEEE-C57.12.90 tolerance. Technician_Lead documents tapchanger contact resistance measured using Vanguard-DV-Power-RMO-D confirming all positions <200 micro-ohms meeting specification for Maschinenfabrik-Reinhausen OILTAP-M tapchanger.

## Circuit Breaker Maintenance

### SF6 Breaker Service
**Gas Monitoring Protocol**: Quarterly inspection of Breaker_52-TL-445A (ABB HPL-B362D, 362 kV, 63 kA interrupting, SF6 insulated) includes gas pressure verification reading 72.3 psig at 20°C reference temperature on primary pressure gauge calibrated annually against NIST-traceable standard. The Maintenance_Specialist uses ABB-GMF-460 gas monitor measuring SF6 purity 99.7% (minimum 98% specification), moisture content 45 ppmv (maximum 150 ppmv at 20°C), and air content 0.2% (maximum 2%) confirming gas quality meets IEC-60480 requirements. Technician performs leak detection using Ion-Science-Tiger handheld SF6 detector (sensitivity 1 ppm) scanning all gasket joints, bushing interfaces, and operating mechanism seals detecting zero leaks, annual leakage rate calculated 0.3% (acceptable <1% per IEC-62271-203).

**Breaker Timing Tests**: Test_Engineer conducts circuit breaker timing analysis on Breaker_52-TL-556B (Siemens 3AP-1 air-blast, 345 kV, 40 kA interrupting) using Megger-EGIL programma timing analyzer measuring contact timing across three phases. The test results show Phase-A open time 2.8 cycles (46.7 milliseconds), Phase-B open time 2.9 cycles (48.3 milliseconds), Phase-C open time 2.7 cycles (45.0 milliseconds) with maximum pole scatter 3.3 milliseconds (specification <5 milliseconds per IEEE-C37.09). Close operation timing measured: Phase-A 3.1 cycles, Phase-B 3.2 cycles, Phase-C 3.0 cycles with close time spread 3.3 milliseconds. Engineer validates operating mechanism energy measured through travel recording showing Stored_Energy_Spring tension 3,450 lbf (specification 3,200-3,600 lbf range) and operating motor current 8.9 A during spring charging cycle.

### Vacuum Breaker Maintenance
**Contact Wear Assessment**: Technician performs vacuum integrity test on Breaker_52-34A (GE PowerVac-38, 38 kV, 40 kA vacuum interrupter) using Megger-VLF-60 very-low-frequency hipot tester applying 42 kV test voltage across open breaker contacts (60% of 70 kV rated BIL). The test duration 1-minute measures leakage current <5 microamps indicating vacuum integrity maintained (acceptable <10 microamps). Maintenance_Supervisor reviews breaker operation counter showing 4,567 operations accumulated (rated 10,000 mechanical operations, 45.7% life consumed) and 234 fault interruptions (rated 100 fault interruptions at maximum 40 kA, estimated 23.4% electrical life consumed based on duty calculations per IEEE-C37.04).

**Mechanical Operation Test**: Field_Technician operates Breaker_52-34A through 10 close-open cycles measuring operating mechanism performance using Megger-BITE-3 battery impedance tester monitoring Control_Battery_125VDC voltage during operation. The battery voltage dips from 127.5V steady-state to 118.3V during close operation (9.2V depression, acceptable <15V per specification) recovering to 126.8V within 2 seconds. Trip coil current measured 12.3 A peak with duration 45 milliseconds, close coil current 23.4 A peak with duration 89 milliseconds both within manufacturer specifications for GE PowerVac control circuits. Mechanical inspection reveals contact travel 14.2 mm (specification 13-15 mm range), contact wipe 2.1 mm (specification >1.5 mm), and spring force 890 N measured using calibrated force gauge.

## Protective Relay Testing

### Relay Calibration Schedule
**Distance Relay Testing**: Protection_Technician tests SEL-421 distance relay protecting Transmission_Line_TL-445 (345 kV, 123 km) using Doble-F6150 relay test set injecting simulated fault quantities validating Zone-1 impedance setting 0.85 ohms (90% of line impedance 0.944 ohms) with instantaneous trip time target 0.5 cycles. The test sequence applies phase-A-to-ground fault at 85% line location (impedance 0.803 ohms) verifying relay trip in 0.48 cycles, applies fault at 95% location (impedance 0.897 ohms) verifying no Zone-1 trip with Zone-2 pickup confirmed after 25-cycle time delay. Test_Engineer validates communication-assisted scheme sending permissive trip signal through SEL-2488 GPS-based pilot channel to remote terminal relay achieving total fault clearing time 1.2 cycles (0.5 cycle local relay + 0.7 cycle communication and remote relay).

**Transformer Differential Testing**: Using Omicron-CMC-356 relay tester, Test_Specialist validates Siemens-7UT613 transformer differential protection on Transformer_Unit_T-556 (Siemens 400 MVA, 345/138 kV, Dyn1 vector group) applying simulated internal fault currents through all six CT inputs (three primary-side 2000:5 CTs rated 345 kV, three secondary-side 1200:5 CTs rated 138 kV). The relay operates at 87% differential current threshold (1,045 A secondary, representing 6,270 A primary internal fault) with operate time 0.6 cycles, harmonic restraint blocks operation during inrush condition simulated by 45% second-harmonic content, and winding temperature element alarm triggers at 105°C simulated RTD resistance input 143.8 ohms representing Qualitrol-Temp-Monitor reading.

### Relay Settings Validation
**Overcurrent Coordination**: Protection_Engineer validates Distribution_Feeder_45A protection coordination using ASPEN-OneLiner software modeling feeder circuit (12.47 kV, 23 km, overhead construction, maximum fault 4,890 A at source). The study confirms SEL-751A feeder relay time-overcurrent element set Curve-5 ANSI characteristic with pickup 480 A (8× normal load 60 A) coordinates with downstream Cooper-Form-6 recloser set 240 A instantaneous, 60 A time-delayed (Curve-101 characteristic), and lateral fuses Cooper-T-40K rated 40 A. Coordination analysis shows minimum coordination interval 0.23 seconds at maximum fault current between feeder relay backup time 1.45 seconds and recloser slow curve time 1.22 seconds meeting IEEE-242 recommended 0.2-second minimum margin.

**Generator Protection Verification**: Test_Engineer verifies SEL-387E generator differential protection on Generator_Unit_GT-778 (Siemens SGT-800, 47 MW, 13.8 kV) applying test currents through ABB-CHB current transformers (3000:5 ratio) validating differential element operates 50% slope characteristic at operate current threshold. The test confirms stator ground protection (64G element) set 5 A primary (8.3 mA secondary through distribution transformer grounding 13.8kV:240V, 20:1 ratio to sensitive ground relay) operates in 0.5 seconds, negative sequence protection (46 element) set 10% continuous 15% trip with 10-second delay, and loss-of-field protection (40 element) offset mho characteristic R=-0.5 per-unit X=1.0 per-unit coordinates with excitation system minimum excitation limiter at AVR Unitrol-6000.

## Equipment Condition Monitoring

### Thermographic Inspection Program
**Infrared Survey Execution**: Thermography_Technician performs annual infrared survey of Substation_Metro (230/138/34.5 kV, 23 circuit positions) using FLIR-T640 thermal imaging camera (640×480 resolution, temperature range -40°C to +2000°C, sensitivity 0.035°C) identifying thermal anomalies indicating equipment deterioration. The survey detects elevated temperature 89°C on Disconnect_Switch_89-C blade contact (ambient 23°C, delta-T 66°C) compared to adjacent phase temperatures 35°C indicating high-resistance contact requiring maintenance. Additional findings include Bus_Connector_BC-445 temperature 78°C (delta-T 55°C, severity Level-2 per NFPA-70B requiring scheduled maintenance) and Distribution_Cable_Termination_T-34A temperature 92°C (delta-T 69°C, severity Level-3 requiring priority maintenance within 2 weeks).

**Ultrasonic Testing**: Using UE-Systems-Ultraprobe-15000 ultrasonic detector, Inspector_Senior performs corona and partial discharge detection survey at Substation_Clearwater identifying electrical discharge activity on Transmission_Bushing_345kV-T445H showing 65 dB ultrasonic level (background 28 dB) indicating internal bushing deterioration. The inspector correlates ultrasonic readings with UV camera imaging using Ofil-DayCor-Superb corona camera detecting visible corona discharge at bushing cap. Follow-up power factor test using Doble-M4000 measures bushing C1 capacitance 445 pF (nameplate 450 pF, 1.1% deviation acceptable) but power factor 2.3% (normal <1.0%) confirming bushing insulation degradation requiring bushing replacement scheduled within 3-month maintenance window.

### Vibration Monitoring
**Rotating Equipment Analysis**: Vibration_Analyst monitors Generator_Unit_GT-445 (GE 7FA.05 turbine, 225 MW, 3,600 rpm) using permanently installed Bently-Nevada 3500/42M proximitor system measuring radial shaft vibration at bearing locations. The monitoring system displays Bearing_1_Vibration 2.8 mils peak-to-peak (alarm threshold 4.0 mils, trip threshold 6.0 mils), Bearing_2_Vibration 3.1 mils, Bearing_3_Vibration 2.6 mils, and Bearing_4_Vibration 2.9 mils all within acceptable range <4.0 mils per API-670 machinery protection standard. Spectrum analysis using Bently-Nevada System-1 software shows dominant vibration component 1× running speed (60 Hz) indicating normal unbalance, secondary component 2× running speed (120 Hz) at 0.8 mils indicating minor misalignment within acceptance criteria.

**Trending and Diagnostics**: Condition_Monitoring_Engineer analyzes vibration trends over 12-month period using OSI-PI trending showing Generator_Unit_GT-445 Bearing_2 vibration increase from baseline 2.1 mils to current 3.1 mils (1.0 mil increase, 48% rise) suggesting developing bearing wear. The engineer reviews lubricating oil analysis from Polaris-Laboratory showing iron content 45 ppm (normal <30 ppm), copper 12 ppm (normal <10 ppm), and viscosity 28.9 cSt at 40°C (specification 28-32 cSt) supporting vibration trend indicating bearing deterioration. Maintenance_Planner schedules bearing inspection during next planned outage (scheduled 6 weeks) with replacement parts Bearing_Tilting-Pad-Journal (Kingsbury design, 5-pad, 18-inch diameter) ordered for potential replacement based on inspection findings.
